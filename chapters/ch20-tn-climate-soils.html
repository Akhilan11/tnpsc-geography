CHAPTER 20 / TAMIL NADU — CLIMATE & SOILS

Tamil Nadu — Climate & Soils.

The only Indian state where the NE monsoon is the main monsoon — and a state whose soils carry every major Indian type.

OBJECTIVES

  • Recall TN's climate type and seasonal pattern.
  • Distinguish TN's NE monsoon dependency from the rest of India.
  • Memorise the major soil types of TN and their distribution.
  • Identify natural vegetation, forest cover, and wildlife reserves.

Climate of Tamil Nadu.

Tamil Nadu's climate is tropical, with three distinct features: heat all year round, rainfall concentrated in the NE monsoon, and high coastal humidity. Average temperatures range from 18°C (winter, hill stations) to 43°C+ (summer, plains).

  • Tropic of Cancer: Does not pass through TN; the state lies entirely south of it — explaining the perennial warmth.
  • Mean annual rainfall: ~ 945 mm (TN avg) — lower than India's average (~ 1,170 mm).
  • Rainfall split: NE monsoon ~ 48%, SW monsoon ~ 35%, summer ~ 12%, winter ~ 5%.

Four seasons in Tamil Nadu.

SeasonMonthsNotes
Cold weather / winterDec – FebTail of NE monsoon. Tail rainfall over Kanyakumari, Tirunelveli, Tuticorin. Mean min 18-22°C.
Hot weather / summerMar – MayHottest month: May. Plains 38-43°C; sometimes > 45°C in interior (Karur, Madurai). Mango showers in some hills.
SW Monsoon (Advancing)Jun – SepMost of TN is in rain shadow of Western Ghats. WG side (Nilgiris, Anaimalai) gets heavy rain; plains relatively dry. About 35% of rainfall.
NE Monsoon (Retreating, Main)Oct – DecThe chief rainy season for TN; ~ 48% of annual rainfall. Cyclones from Bay of Bengal common Oct–Dec. Chennai's wettest month: November.

Rainfall distribution within TN.

  • Wettest: Western parts — Nilgiris (Coonoor, Cherambadi receive > 2,500 mm), Anaimalai, Cardamom Hills. The Nilgiris hill stations resemble Kerala in rainfall.
  • Driest: Interior plateau — Coimbatore (~ 650 mm), Madurai (~ 800 mm), Tiruchi.
  • Highest in plains: Coastal districts — Nagapattinam, Cuddalore, Tiruvarur (~ 1,300-1,500 mm).
  • Lowest in TN: Coimbatore-Erode rain shadow of the Anaimalais (~ 600 mm).
KEY FACT — TAMIL CALENDAR

The traditional Tamil calendar names the rainy season months: Aippasi (Oct-Nov) = onset of NE monsoon; Karthigai (Nov-Dec) = peak; Margazhi (Dec-Jan) = post-monsoon. The agricultural calendar — Sornavari, Samba, Navarai/Thaladi — is calibrated to this rainfall pattern.

Cyclones and disasters.

Tamil Nadu's coast is among the most cyclone-prone in India. Memorable recent cyclones:

  • 1996 Andhra-Tamil Nadu cyclone — devastated southern AP & northern TN.
  • 2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami — December 26; ~ 8,000 dead in TN, esp. Nagapattinam, Karaikal, Velankanni.
  • 2011 Cyclone Thane — December; Cuddalore, Puducherry hit hard.
  • 2015 Chennai Floods — Dec 2015; one of India's costliest urban disasters.
  • 2016 Cyclone Vardah — Chennai-Tiruvallur.
  • 2018 Cyclone Gaja — Nagapattinam, Tiruvarur, Pudukkottai. Massive coconut plantation loss.
  • 2018 Cyclone Ockhi — Kanyakumari coast.
  • 2020 Cyclone Nivar — Cuddalore.
  • 2023 Cyclone Michaung — Chennai floods December 2023.

TAMIL NADU CONNECTION — SOIL DEGRADATION

TN faces three soil challenges: salinity (Cauvery delta over-irrigation, coastal intrusion), erosion (deforestation in Western Ghats), and fertility loss (intensive paddy monoculture). The TN State Department of Agriculture issues soil health cards under the central scheme since 2015.

Forest cover (ISFR 2023)

  • Total forest area of TN: 26,419 km² (about 20.3% of TN's area).
  • National average: 21.76%. TN is slightly below.
  • Districts with highest forest cover: Nilgiris, Dharmapuri, Erode, Coimbatore.
  • Mangrove cover: ~ 45 km² (Pichavaram and Muthupet are the principal sites).

National parks, sanctuaries, biosphere & tiger reserves in Tamil Nadu.

National Parks (5 in TN)

  1. Mudumalai National Park — Nilgiris; declared NP 1990, sanctuary 1940. Tiger, elephant.
  2. Mukurthi National Park — Nilgiris. Nilgiri Tahr (state animal).
  3. Anaimalai (Indira Gandhi) National Park — Coimbatore-Tiruppur. Tiger, lion-tailed macaque.
  4. Guindy National Park — Chennai. One of smallest NPs in India; blackbuck, spotted deer.
  5. Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park — Ramanathapuram-Thoothukudi. 21 islands; dugong, sea turtles.

Tiger Reserves in TN (5)

  1. Kalakkad-Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve — Tirunelveli/Tenkasi. Largest in TN.
  2. Anaimalai Tiger Reserve — Coimbatore-Tiruppur.
  3. Mudumalai Tiger Reserve — Nilgiris.
  4. Sathyamangalam Tiger Reserve — Erode. Notified 2013.
  5. Srivilliputhur-Megamalai Tiger Reserve — Virudhunagar/Theni. Notified 2021.

Biosphere Reserves with TN parts

  • Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve — India's first, 1986; spans TN, Kerala, Karnataka. Includes Mudumalai, Bandipur, Nagarhole, Mukurthi, Silent Valley.
  • Gulf of Mannar Biosphere — 1989; India's first marine biosphere.
  • Agasthyamalai Biosphere — 2001; TN-Kerala.

Major wildlife sanctuaries in TN

  • Vedanthangal Bird Sanctuary — Chengalpattu. Oldest bird sanctuary in India (early 18th c.).
  • Karikili Bird Sanctuary — Chengalpattu (near Vedanthangal).
  • Point Calimere Wildlife Sanctuary — Nagapattinam. Flamingos, blackbuck.
  • Kanyakumari Wildlife Sanctuary — Kanyakumari.
  • Mukurthi NP & Mudumalai sanctuaries — Nilgiris.
  • Vettangudi Bird Sanctuary — Sivagangai.
  • Kanjirankulam, Chitrangudi — Ramanathapuram bird sanctuaries.
  • Pulicat Bird Sanctuary — Tiruvallur (TN side).
  • Pichavaram Mangrove — Cuddalore.
  • Indira Gandhi Wildlife Sanctuary — Coimbatore (around Anaimalai NP).
QUICK RECAP

What this chapter said.

  • TN is tropical with the unique feature: NE monsoon (Oct-Dec) is the main rainfall season — ~ 48% of annual rain.
  • Mean annual rainfall: ~ 945 mm. Wettest: Nilgiris (> 2,500 mm). Driest: Coimbatore rain shadow (~ 600 mm).
  • Recent cyclones: 2004 Tsunami, 2011 Thane, 2015 Floods, 2018 Gaja, 2023 Michaung.
  • Soils: red (65%), black, alluvial (Cauvery delta), laterite (Nilgiris), coastal saline, peaty, forest.
  • 5 National Parks: Mudumalai, Mukurthi, Anaimalai (IG), Guindy, Gulf of Mannar.
  • 5 Tiger Reserves: Kalakkad-Mundanthurai, Anaimalai, Mudumalai, Sathyamangalam, Srivilliputhur-Megamalai.
  • 3 Biosphere Reserves: Nilgiri (India's first), Gulf of Mannar (1st marine), Agasthyamalai.
  • Vedanthangal — oldest bird sanctuary in India.

01The main rainfall season for Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Cold weather (Dec-Feb)
  2. Summer (Mar-May)
  3. SW monsoon (Jun-Sep)
  4. NE retreating monsoon (Oct-Dec)
Reveal answer

D — NE retreating monsoon (Oct-Dec). ~ 48% of annual rainfall.

02The driest part of Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Nagapattinam coast
  2. Coimbatore plateau (rain shadow)
  3. Nilgiris
  4. Chennai
Reveal answer

B — Coimbatore-Erode rain shadow of the Anaimalais.

03TN's first Tiger Reserve (notified 1988) is —
  1. Mudumalai
  2. Anaimalai
  3. Kalakkad-Mundanthurai
  4. Sathyamangalam
Reveal answer

C — Kalakkad-Mundanthurai.

  1. Chennai
  2. Nagapattinam & Cauvery delta
  3. Kanyakumari
  4. Coimbatore

05The dominant soil type of Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Alluvial
