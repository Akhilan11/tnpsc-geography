CHAPTER 19 / TAMIL NADU — PHYSIOGRAPHY & DRAINAGE

Tamil Nadu —
Physiography & Drainage.

Hills in the west, plains in the east, a long coast in between — and the rivers that knit them together.

OBJECTIVES

  • Identify TN's four physiographic divisions.
  • Map the hills of Western and Eastern Ghats within TN.
  • Recall the major rivers, their tributaries, and the dams on them.
  • Locate coastal features — lagoons, ports, gulfs, channels.

Four physiographic divisions of Tamil Nadu.

  1. The Western Ghats (Sahyadri) — high hills along the western border.
  2. The Eastern Ghats — discontinuous hills in the interior.
  3. The interior plateau — between the two Ghats; includes Kongu and Salem plateau.
  4. The Eastern Coastal Plain (Coromandel) — along the Bay of Bengal coast.

MNEMONIC — EASTERN GHATS HILLS IN TN

"JeYS-KK-PaBC" (Javadhu, Yelagiri, Shevaroy, Kalrayan, Kolli, Pachaimalai, Bodamalai, Chitteri). Most are in Salem/Namakkal/Vellore districts; spice and coffee cultivation; small hill stations.

Plateaus & uplands.

Between the Western and Eastern Ghats lies the interior plateau of Tamil Nadu, comprising:

  • Coimbatore Plateau (Kongu Nadu) — between Palghat Gap and Nilgiris; agricultural and industrial heart; Bhavani & Amaravati rivers.
  • Salem Plateau — east of Coimbatore; iron, magnesite, bauxite.
  • Madurai Plateau — Vaigai river; sugarcane.
  • Tirunelveli (Tambraparani) basin — south.

Plains.

The most significant plain of TN is the Cauvery delta — the rice bowl. Includes Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam, Mayiladuthurai districts; parts of Tiruchi, Cuddalore, Pudukkottai. Other significant plains: Palar plain (north), Vaigai plain (Madurai), Tamiraparani plain (Tirunelveli).

Rivers of Tamil Nadu.

TN has 17 major river basins. All flow generally west-to-east except where the Ghats divert them. Rivers are mostly seasonal (NE monsoon-fed) with one notable perennial — the Cauvery.

The Cauvery (Kaveri) — TN's lifeblood

Already covered in Chapter 8 in detail. In TN:

  • Enters TN from Karnataka via Hogenakkal Falls (Dharmapuri district).
  • Flows through Erode, Karur, Tiruchirappalli, Thanjavur districts.
  • Splits into Cauvery (south) and Kollidam (Coleroon) (north) at Srirangam.
  • Drains into the Bay at Poompuhar (Kaveripattinam), Mayiladuthurai district.
  • Major tributaries in TN: Bhavani, Noyyal, Amaravathi (right bank); Kabini, Suvarnavathi (right, but upstream in KA); Kollidam (north).

TAMIL NADU CONNECTION — POWER FROM PYKARA

Tamil Nadu's first hydroelectric station was at Pykara (Nilgiris, 1932). The Pykara-Mukurthi-Glenmorgan group of stations established Nilgiris as a major hydropower zone. Mettur was added in 1937. Today the Kundah hydroelectric complex remains a major source of TN's hydro power.

QUICK RECAP

What this chapter said.

  • TN physiographic divisions: WG (west), EG (interior, discontinuous), plateau (between), coastal plain (east).
  • WG-TN hills: Nilgiris (Doddabetta), Anaimalai, Palani (Vandaravu), Cardamom, Varushanad, Sirumalai.
  • EG-TN hills: Javadhu, Yelagiri, Shevaroy, Kalrayan, Kolli, Pachaimalai, Bodamalai, Chitteri.
  • 17 major river basins; Cauvery (perennial) + 16 mostly seasonal.
  • Major rivers: Palar, Pennaiyar, Vellar, Cauvery, Kollidam, Vaigai, Tamiraparani, Bhavani, Amaravathi, Noyyal.
  • Cauvery enters TN at Hogenakkal; splits at Srirangam; reaches sea at Poompuhar.
  • Tamiraparani is the only perennial river of southern TN.
  • Major dams: Mettur (1934, Cauvery), Bhavanisagar, Amaravathi, Sathanur, Vaigai, Manimuthar, Papanasam, Mullaperiyar, Pykara (first hydro 1932).
  • Coast: 1,076 km. Major coastal features: Pulicat, Marina, Mahabalipuram, Pichavaram, Pamban, Adam's Bridge, Kanyakumari.

Practice MCQs

CH 19 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The highest peak of the Nilgiris is —
  1. Mukurthi
  2. Doddabetta
  3. Anaimudi
  4. Vandaravu
Reveal answer

B — Doddabetta (2,637 m). Anaimudi is in Kerala.

02"Kodaikanal", the Princess of Hill Stations, is in which hills?
  1. Nilgiris
  2. Anaimalai
  3. Palani
  4. Shevaroy
Reveal answer

C — Palani Hills (Dindigul district).

03Yercaud hill station is on which range?
  1. Kalrayan
  2. Shevaroy
  3. Kolli
  4. Pachaimalai
Reveal answer

B — Shevaroy (Servarayan) Hills, Salem.

04The only perennial river of southern Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Vaigai
  2. Tamiraparani
  3. Vaippar
  4. Gundar
Reveal answer

B — Tamiraparani (Tirunelveli).

05The Mettur Dam was completed in —
  1. 1929
  2. 1934
  3. 1959
  4. 1980
Reveal answer

B — 1934. Creates Stanley Reservoir on the Cauvery.

06The Cauvery enters Tamil Nadu through which falls/town?
  1. Mettur
  2. Hogenakkal
  3. Bhavani
  4. Tirumakudal
Reveal answer

B — Hogenakkal Falls (Dharmapuri district).

07The largest tributary of the Cauvery within Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Noyyal
  2. Bhavani
  3. Amaravathi
  4. Kollidam
Reveal answer

B — Bhavani. Joins at Bhavani town in Erode district.

08"Pichavaram", the second-largest mangrove in India, is in which district?
  1. Cuddalore
  2. Nagapattinam
  3. Mayiladuthurai
  4. Thanjavur
Reveal answer

A — Cuddalore.

09The Vaigai river drains into —
  1. Arabian Sea
  2. Palk Bay / Bay of Bengal
  3. Gulf of Mannar
  4. Joins Cauvery
Reveal answer

B — Palk Bay, at Ramanathapuram.

10Tamil Nadu's first hydroelectric power station (1932) is at —
  1. Mettur
  2. Pykara
  3. Kundah
  4. Sathanur
Reveal answer

B — Pykara (Nilgiris).
