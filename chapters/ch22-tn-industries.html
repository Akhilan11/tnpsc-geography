CHAPTER 22 / TAMIL NADU — INDUSTRIES & ENERGY

Tamil Nadu —
Industries & Energy.

India's most industrialised southern state — autos in Chennai, textiles in Tiruppur, leather in Vellore, IT along OMR, and wind on the Kanyakumari coast.

OBJECTIVES

  • Understand TN's industrial profile and ranking.
  • Identify the key clusters: autos, textiles, leather, IT, engineering, food processing.
  • Locate the major industrial corridors and SEZs.
  • Recall TN's mineral and energy resources.

Industrial overview.

  • TN is the 2nd most industrialised state in India (after Maharashtra), contributing about 10%+ of national GVA in manufacturing.
  • Per Annual Survey of Industries, TN has the highest number of factories in India.
  • TN GSDP is among top three in India.
  • Key clusters: automobiles, textiles, leather, IT & electronics, engineering, food processing, pharmaceuticals.

Automobile industry — "Detroit of India".

Chennai is widely called the "Detroit of India" (or "Detroit of South Asia"). The Chennai-Sriperumbudur-Oragadam-Hosur belt produces roughly 30% of India's passenger cars and 35% of commercial vehicles.

Auto components

TN hosts ~ 30% of India's auto component manufacturers. Key clusters: Chennai, Coimbatore, Hosur. Major firms: Sundaram-Clayton, TVS Sundram Fasteners, MRF (Chennai HQ), Brakes India, Wheels India.

Textile industry.

Cotton textiles — Coimbatore the "Manchester of South India"

TN has the largest concentration of cotton textile mills in India. Key centres:

  • Coimbatore — "Manchester of South India"; spinning mills, hosiery.
  • Tiruppur — "Knitwear City of India"; ~ 90% of India's cotton knitwear exports.
  • Erode — textile processing, dyeing.
  • Karur — home textiles, handloom (Karur cotton fabrics).
  • Salem — silver work + Salem silk.
  • Madurai & Madurai Sungudi — traditional dyeing.
  • Kanchipuram — silk sarees (GI tag).
  • Arani — silk weaving.
  • Chennai (Avadi, Ambattur) — garment exports.

Silk & handlooms

Kanchipuram silk, Arani silk, Madurai Sungudi cotton, Coimbatore Cora cotton, Salem silk all have GI tags. TN has the largest number of handloom weavers in India.

Leather industry.

TN produces ~ 60% of India's leather and ~ 40% of leather exports. Cluster centred on:

  • Vellore-Ranipet-Ambur — North TN leather belt.
  • Dindigul — South TN leather.
  • Chennai-Pallavaram — finished leather goods.

The Central Leather Research Institute (CLRI) is headquartered in Chennai.

IT, ITeS, electronics.

TN is India's 2nd largest IT exporter (after Karnataka). Key hubs:

  • Chennai (OMR / Old Mahabalipuram Road) — "IT Expressway"; campuses of TCS, Infosys, Cognizant, Wipro, HCL, Tech Mahindra, Accenture, Capgemini, Standard Chartered, Bank of America. Plus DLF, RMZ, TIDEL Parks.
  • Coimbatore — "TIDEL Park Coimbatore"; rising 2nd hub.
  • Tiruchirappalli — emerging IT centre.
  • Madurai — IT park.
  • Hosur — straddles Bengaluru-Chennai IT corridor.

Electronics manufacturing

TN is India's largest electronics exporter. Sriperumbudur hosts Foxconn's Apple iPhone factory; Pegatron (Apple supplier); Salcomp (chargers). Other key plants: Nokia (former, now sold), Samsung (small operations), Dell, Flex, Sanmina.

Engineering, defence, aerospace.

  • BHEL Tiruchirappalli — boilers, power equipment; one of India's largest engineering plants.
  • HVF Avadi (Heavy Vehicles Factory, OFB) — tanks (T-90, Arjun MBT).
  • Engine Factory Avadi (EFA) — tank engines.
  • Ordnance Factory Tiruchi — small arms.
  • Integral Coach Factory (ICF) Perambur, Chennai — manufactures train coaches; Vande Bharat is built here.
  • HAL Engine Plant, Bengaluru, draws TN supply chain.
  • Defence Industrial Corridor — TN node (Tiruchi-Coimbatore-Salem-Chennai-Hosur).

Other significant industries.

  • Sugar mills — Pugalur, Anaikulam, Padalur, Annamalai, Subramaniya Siva (Cuddalore).
  • Cement — Dalmiapuram (Tiruchi), Ariyalur (largest cement zone in TN — Madras Cements, Dalmia, India Cements), Sankaridurg (Salem).
  • Paper — Tamil Nadu Newsprint and Papers Ltd (TNPL), Karur; Seshasayee Paper, Erode.
  • Petrochemicals — Manali (Chennai) — Chennai Petroleum Corporation Ltd (CPCL). Madras Fertilizers (MFL), Manali.
  • Refineries — CPCL Manali; Nagapattinam (Cauvery basin refinery).
  • Pharmaceuticals — Chennai, Pondicherry (UT, but supply chain).
  • Sago / starch — Salem (largest sago industry cluster in India).
  • Fireworks & matches — Sivakasi (Virudhunagar) — produces ~ 90% of India's firecrackers.
  • Match boxes & printing — Sivakasi also dominates.
  • Granite — Salem, Krishnagiri, Madurai, Karur (black, red, multicolour granites — major export).
  • Salt — Marakkanam, Vedaranyam, Tuticorin.
KEY FACT — SIVAKASI

Sivakasi (Virudhunagar) produces ~ 90% of India's firecrackers, ~ 90% of safety matches, and significant printing & lithography output. The town's three industries (Cracker, Match, Print) make it one of India's most concentrated industrial clusters.

Industrial corridors & SEZs.

  • Chennai-Bengaluru Industrial Corridor (CBIC) — DMIC's southern counterpart; Sriperumbudur, Ranipet, Krishnagiri, Hosur.
  • Vizag-Chennai Industrial Corridor (VCIC) — east coast.
  • Madurai-Tuticorin Industrial Corridor — port-centric.
  • SEZs in TN — Largest concentration in India; OMR Chennai (IT), Sriperumbudur (electronics), Nokia (Sriperumbudur), Hosur, MEPZ (Madras Export Processing Zone — Tambaram), Coimbatore.

Energy in Tamil Nadu.

TN's total installed power capacity (2024): ~ 37 GW. Mix: thermal ~ 38%, renewables (mainly wind & solar) ~ 45%, nuclear ~ 6%, hydro ~ 6%, gas ~ 5%. Tamil Nadu is the largest installer of wind power in India.

Thermal power

  • Neyveli Lignite Corporation (NLC) — multiple thermal stations using Neyveli lignite. Cuddalore district.
  • North Chennai Thermal Power Station — Ennore.
  • Mettur Thermal Power Station — Salem.
  • Tuticorin Thermal Power Station — Thoothukudi.
  • Vallur, Udangudi, Ennore SEZ — under construction / new.

Nuclear

  • Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant (Tirunelveli) — Russian-tech VVER reactors; India's largest nuclear capacity site (6,000 MW planned, 2,000 MW operational).
  • Kalpakkam (Madras Atomic Power Station — MAPS) — Kanchipuram district. Plus the prototype Fast Breeder Reactor (PFBR), and Indira Gandhi Centre for Atomic Research (IGCAR).

Hydro

  • Mettur — Cauvery.
  • Bhavanisagar & Lower Bhavani — Bhavani.
  • Kundah complex — Nilgiris (multi-stage hydro).
  • Pykara, Mukurthi, Glenmorgan — Nilgiris.
  • Periyar (Mullaperiyar) — for power generation by TN.
  • Papanasam, Manimuthar — Tirunelveli (small hydro).

Wind

TN was the first state in India to cross 10 GW of wind. Main wind corridors:

  • Muppandal (Kanyakumari) — once the world's largest onshore wind farm cluster.
  • Aralvaimozhi pass (Kanyakumari) — natural wind funnel between WG and the Indian Ocean.
  • Coimbatore — Anaimalai foothills, also a wind corridor.
  • Theni, Tirunelveli, Tuticorin — major wind zones.

Solar

  • Kamuthi Solar Power Project — Ramanathapuram (648 MW, commissioned 2016); was the largest single-location solar park in the world at the time.
  • Tutucorin Solar Park, Theni, Virudhunagar, Sivagangai — major solar zones.
