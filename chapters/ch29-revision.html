Exam-day strategy.

TNPSC EXAM PLAYBOOK

The night before — Skim this chapter. Don't try to study new material. Sleep early.

The morning of — Light food. Re-read the "50 facts" table once.

In the exam:
1. Geography questions tend to be factual — answer them first when you scan the paper, they're your fastest marks.
2. For "which of the following" questions, eliminate two options first, then choose between the remaining two.
3. If a statement involves a year or a number, look for that anchor — usually the wrong options use plausible but incorrect numbers.
4. For map-based questions, draw a quick rough sketch in the margin.
5. Don't skip — geography negative marking is usually 1/3 or 1/4 mark. If you can eliminate one option, attempt; if you can't eliminate any, skip.
6. Read the question twice — many TNPSC questions hinge on a single word ("not", "except", "all of the above").
