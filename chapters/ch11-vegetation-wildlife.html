CHAPTER 11 / VEGETATION & WILDLIFE

Natural Vegetation & Wildlife.

From the world's largest mangrove forest to the cold deserts of Ladakh — India hosts more biodiversity per square kilometre than any other comparable land.

OBJECTIVES

  • Identify the five major forest types under Champion's classification.
  • Recall the latest Forest Survey of India statistics on forest cover.
  • Distinguish national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, biosphere reserves, and tiger reserves.
  • Memorise the major reserves of India with location and signature species.
  • Name the main wildlife conservation projects and their landmark years.

India lies in two of the world's 17 megadiverse countries (with countries like Brazil, China, Indonesia). Within its 2.4% of global land, India houses about 8% of all species. Four of the world's 36 biodiversity hotspots intersect India: the Himalaya, Indo-Burma, Western Ghats & Sri Lanka, and Sundaland (Nicobar Islands). The classification system used for Indian forests is the one devised by H. G. Champion in 1936, later revised with S. K. Seth in 1968.

KEY FACT — MOST WIDESPREAD

Tropical deciduous (monsoon) forests cover the largest area of India. The Sundarbans in West Bengal is the world's largest mangrove forest.

Forest cover statistics — ISFR 2023.

Source: India State of Forest Report 2023, Forest Survey of India.

  • Total forest cover: ~ 7,15,343 km² = 21.76% of India's geographical area.
  • Total tree cover (forest + tree outside forest): ~ 25.17%.
  • National Forest Policy 1988 target: 33% of total geographic area (66% for hill regions).
  • State with largest forest area: Madhya Pradesh.
  • State with highest percentage of forest cover: Mizoram (over 84%).
  • States with notable mangrove cover: West Bengal (Sundarbans), Gujarat (Kachchh), Andaman & Nicobar.

Wildlife profile.

India is home to about 75 species of mammals, 1,300 species of birds, hundreds of reptiles, amphibians, and arthropods. Iconic species: Bengal tiger (national animal), Indian elephant (national heritage animal), Asiatic lion (Gir, Gujarat — only wild population in the world), Indian rhinoceros (Kaziranga & Manas), snow leopard (Himalayan), Indian peacock (national bird), Indian bustard, Nilgiri tahr, Hangul (Kashmir stag).

Wildlife Sanctuaries.

India has over 567 wildlife sanctuaries. Key ones:

  • Vedanthangal (TN) — oldest bird sanctuary in India (estd. early 18th century by local farmers).
  • Mudumalai (TN) — established 1940, one of the earliest in S India.
  • Bharatpur / Keoladeo (Rajasthan) — UNESCO; key wintering ground for Siberian cranes.
  • Chilika (Odisha) — flamingos, Irrawaddy dolphins.
  • Periyar (Kerala) — tiger and elephant.
  • Pulicat (TN-AP) — flamingos.
  • Point Calimere (TN) — flamingos, blackbuck.
  • Wild Ass Sanctuary (Little Rann, Gujarat) — only home of Indian wild ass (khur).

Biosphere Reserves.

India has 18 biosphere reserves, 12 of which are part of UNESCO's World Network. Key ones:

  1. Nilgiri (TN, Kerala, Karnataka) — first biosphere reserve in India, 1986. Mudumalai + Bandipur + Wayanad + Nagarhole + Mukurthi + Silent Valley.
  2. Nanda Devi (Uttarakhand) — 1988
  3. Nokrek (Meghalaya) — 1988
  4. Gulf of Mannar (TN) — 1989, India's first marine BR
  5. Sundarbans (WB) — 1989
  6. Manas (Assam) — 1989
  7. Great Nicobar — 1989
  8. Similipal (Odisha) — 1994
  9. Dibru-Saikhowa (Assam) — 1997
  10. Dehang-Debang (Arunachal) — 1998
  11. Pachmarhi (MP) — 1999
  12. Khangchendzonga (Sikkim) — 2000
  13. Agasthyamalai (TN-Kerala) — 2001
  14. Achanakmar-Amarkantak (MP-Chhattisgarh) — 2005
  15. Kachchh (Gujarat) — largest in India, 2008
  16. Cold Desert (Himachal) — 2009
  17. Seshachalam (AP) — 2010
  18. Panna (MP) — 2011

Tiger Reserves & Project Tiger.

Project Tiger was launched in 1973 by Indira Gandhi at Jim Corbett. India now has 56 tiger reserves (2024 figure). India's tiger population reached 3,682 in the 2022 census (latest All India Tiger Estimation by NTCA) — over 75% of the world's wild tiger population. Madhya Pradesh has the highest tiger count.

Tamil Nadu's tiger reserves: Kalakkad-Mundanthurai (largest in TN), Anaimalai (Indira Gandhi), Mudumalai, Sathyamangalam, Srivilliputhur-Megamalai.

TAMIL NADU CONNECTION

TN's protected area network is among the densest in India. Key sites you must memorise: Mudumalai & Anaimalai NPs, Mukurthi NP (Nilgiri tahr), Guindy NP (Chennai), Marine NP Gulf of Mannar, Vedanthangal & Karikili bird sanctuaries, Point Calimere, Sathyamangalam tiger reserve, Kalakkad-Mundanthurai TR, Agasthyamalai BR, Gulf of Mannar BR, Pichavaram mangroves.
