CHAPTER 25 / WORLD · CONTINENTS

Continents at a Glance.

Seven continents, five oceans — a fast tour of the world's major mountains, rivers, deserts, plateaus.

OBJECTIVES

  • Identify the seven continents in order of size.
  • Recall the major mountain ranges, plateaus, deserts, rivers, lakes of each.
  • Memorise key country facts of each continent.
  • Name and locate the five oceans.

Asia.

  • Largest continent; ~ 30% of Earth's land area.
  • Highest point: Mt. Everest (8,848.86 m), Nepal-China.
  • Lowest land point on Earth: Dead Sea (~ 430 m below sea level), Israel-Jordan.
  • Longest river: Yangtze (6,300 km), China.
  • Other major rivers: Yellow (Huang He), Mekong, Brahmaputra, Ganga, Indus, Ob, Yenisei, Lena.
  • Largest lake: Caspian Sea (371,000 km²) — also the largest lake in the world.
  • Largest country: Russia (Asian part) + China.
  • Major mountain systems: Himalayas, Karakoram, Hindu Kush, Pamirs, Tien Shan, Altai, Kunlun, Urals.
  • Major plateaus: Tibetan Plateau (~ 4,500 m, "Roof of the World"), Deccan, Iranian, Anatolian.
  • Major deserts: Gobi, Thar, Arabian, Karakum, Kyzylkum, Taklamakan, Syrian.
  • Major straits: Malacca, Hormuz, Bosphorus, Bering.

Africa.

  • Second largest continent.
  • Highest point: Kilimanjaro (5,895 m), Tanzania.
  • Longest river: Nile (6,650 km), longest in the world.
  • Other major rivers: Congo, Niger, Zambezi, Limpopo, Orange.
  • Largest lake: Lake Victoria — second largest freshwater lake in the world.
  • Largest desert: Sahara (~ 9 million km²) — world's largest hot desert.
  • Other deserts: Kalahari, Namib.
  • Great Rift Valley runs from Lebanon to Mozambique; created the East African Lakes (Tanganyika, Malawi).
  • Crossed by Equator, Tropic of Cancer, Tropic of Capricorn, Prime Meridian — only continent.
  • Most countries: 54.

Europe.

  • Geographically a peninsula of Asia (called "Eurasia"). Separated by the Urals.
  • Highest point: Mt. Elbrus (5,642 m), Russia (Caucasus).
  • Highest in Western Europe: Mont Blanc (4,810 m), French-Italian Alps.
  • Longest river: Volga (3,690 km), Russia.
  • Other major rivers: Danube, Rhine, Thames, Seine, Loire, Po, Dnipro.
  • Largest lake: Lake Ladoga (Russia).
  • Major mountain ranges: Alps, Pyrenees, Apennines, Carpathians, Urals, Scandinavian.
  • Major plains: North European Plain, Russian Plain.
  • Major straits: Strait of Gibraltar (separates Europe from Africa), English Channel.

North America.

  • Third largest continent.
  • Highest point: Denali / Mt. McKinley (6,190 m), Alaska.
  • Lowest: Death Valley (-86 m), California.
  • Longest river system: Mississippi-Missouri (~ 6,275 km).
  • Largest lake: Lake Superior — world's largest freshwater lake by area.
  • Other Great Lakes: Huron, Michigan, Erie, Ontario.
  • Major mountain ranges: Rocky Mountains (W), Appalachian Mountains (E).
  • Major plateaus: Colorado, Mexican.
  • Famous canyon: Grand Canyon (Colorado River, Arizona).
  • Largest country (by area): Canada (2nd in world).
  • Highest waterfall: not in N America — Angel Falls (Venezuela, S America) is world's tallest. Niagara Falls (USA-Canada) is famous.

South America.

  • Highest point: Aconcagua (6,961 m), Argentina — highest peak outside Asia.
  • Longest river: Amazon (~ 6,400 km) — by some measures longer than Nile; by water discharge it's the world's largest.
  • Largest tropical rainforest: Amazon (5.5 million km²).
  • Longest mountain range: Andes (~ 7,000 km), world's longest continental range.
  • Atacama Desert (Chile) — driest place on Earth.
  • Largest country: Brazil (5th in world by area).
  • Highest waterfall in the world: Angel Falls (979 m), Venezuela.
  • Lake Titicaca (Bolivia-Peru) — highest navigable lake (~ 3,812 m).

Australia & Oceania.

  • Smallest continent.
  • Highest point: Mt. Kosciuszko (2,228 m), Australia (mainland); higher in Oceania: Mt. Wilhelm (4,509 m), Papua New Guinea.
  • Longest river: Murray-Darling (~ 3,672 km).
  • Largest lake: Lake Eyre (mostly dry salt pan).
  • Great Barrier Reef — world's largest coral reef system (off Queensland coast).
  • Major desert: Great Victoria, Great Sandy, Gibson.
  • Includes thousands of Pacific islands: Melanesia, Micronesia, Polynesia.

Antarctica.

  • 5th largest continent by area; coldest place on Earth.
  • Lowest temperature ever recorded: -89.2°C at Vostok Station (1983).
  • Highest point: Vinson Massif (4,892 m).
  • South Pole: 90°S, sits on Antarctic plateau ~ 2,835 m.
  • Antarctic Treaty (1959) — area south of 60°S reserved for peaceful, scientific use.
  • India's stations: Maitri (since 1989), Bharati (2012); the earliest Indian station Dakshin Gangotri (1983-1990) is now submerged.

Practice MCQs

CH 25 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The smallest continent by area is —
  1. Europe
  2. Antarctica
  3. Australia/Oceania
  4. South America
Reveal answer

C — Australia/Oceania.

02The longest river in the world is —
  1. Amazon
  2. Nile
  3. Yangtze
  4. Mississippi
Reveal answer

B — Nile (6,650 km). The Amazon is by some measures longer; standard textbook answer is Nile.

03The deepest point of any ocean is —
  1. Java Trench
  2. Puerto Rico Trench
  3. Mariana Trench (Pacific)
  4. Diamantina Deep
Reveal answer

C — Mariana Trench (Challenger Deep, ~ 11,034 m).

04The highest peak outside Asia is —
  1. Kilimanjaro
  2. Aconcagua
  3. Denali
  4. Elbrus
Reveal answer

B — Aconcagua (6,961 m) in the Andes, Argentina.

05The world's largest hot desert is —
  1. Gobi
  2. Arabian
  3. Sahara
  4. Kalahari
Reveal answer

C — Sahara (~ 9 million km²).

06Angel Falls, the world's tallest waterfall, is in —
  1. Brazil
  2. Venezuela
  3. Colombia
  4. Peru
Reveal answer

B — Venezuela (979 m).

07The world's longest continental mountain range is —
  1. Himalayas
  2. Rockies
  3. Andes
  4. Alps
Reveal answer

C — Andes (~ 7,000 km).

08The lowest point on Earth's land surface is —
  1. Death Valley
  2. Dead Sea
  3. Lake Eyre
  4. Mariana Trench
Reveal answer

B — Dead Sea (~ 430 m below sea level). Mariana Trench is the deepest ocean point but not land.

09The Antarctic Treaty was signed in —
  1. 1949
  2. 1959
  3. 1969
  4. 1979
Reveal answer

B — 1959. Reserves the continent for peaceful, scientific use.

10India's current Antarctic research stations are —
  1. Dakshin Gangotri & Maitri
  2. Maitri & Bharati
  3. Bharati & Vikram
  4. Arctic Polar & Antarctic Polar
Reveal answer

B — Maitri (since 1989) & Bharati (2012). Dakshin Gangotri was the first (1983) but is now submerged.
