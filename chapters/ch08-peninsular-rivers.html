CHAPTER 08 / DRAINAGE · PENINSULAR RIVERS

Peninsular Rivers, Lakes & Falls.

The rivers of the south — older than the Himalayas, shorter than them, and the entire basis of Tamil Nadu's civilisation.

OBJECTIVES OF THIS CHAPTER

  • Distinguish east-flowing and west-flowing peninsular rivers and explain why most flow east.
  • Trace the Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Cauvery and identify their major tributaries.
  • Trace the Narmada and Tapi and understand the rift-valley nature of their courses.
  • Recall India's important natural and artificial lakes.
  • Recall major waterfalls — height, river, location.
  • Understand the Cauvery, Krishna, Mullaperiyar, and Mahanadi water disputes.

South of the Vindhyas, the rivers are different in every important way from those of the north. They are short, shallow, mostly seasonal (driven by monsoon rain, not by snowmelt), and they have eroded their courses into hard ancient rock. The peninsular plateau tilts gently east — so almost all the great peninsular rivers flow eastward into the Bay of Bengal and build large deltas. The exceptions are the Narmada and Tapi, which flow westward through rift valleys created by tectonic faulting.

Nature of peninsular rivers.

  1. Mostly seasonal — depend on monsoon rainfall; reduced to trickle in summer.
  2. Mostly consequent — i.e. younger than the slope they flow on (unlike the antecedent Himalayan rivers).
  3. Mature courses — broad, shallow valleys; few meanders.
  4. Lower sediment load than Himalayan rivers.
  5. East-flowing rivers form deltas; west-flowing rivers form estuaries.

East-flowing rivers.

1. Mahanadi

Originates at Sihawa hills, Dhamtari district, Chhattisgarh. Length: ~ 858 km. Flows through Chhattisgarh and Odisha. Major tributaries: Seonath, Tel, Ib, Hasdeo. Discharges into the Bay of Bengal at Paradip, forming a delta. The Hirakud dam on the Mahanadi (built 1957) was India's first major multi-purpose river-valley project and is one of the longest earthen dams in the world (~ 25.8 km including dykes).

2. Godavari — "Dakshin Ganga"

The longest peninsular river: ~ 1,465 km. Source: Trimbak, Nashik, Maharashtra (Western Ghats). Flows east through Maharashtra, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh; discharges into the Bay of Bengal near Rajahmundry, forming a vast delta. The basin covers about 10% of India's area.

Major tributaries (left bank, from the Satpura/Maikal): Penganga, Wardha, Wainganga (combine to form Pranhita) → Indravati → Sabari. Right bank: Pravara, Manjira, Maner. The Wainganga + Wardha combination at Pranhita is the largest tributary; together they almost double the river. The Polavaram project in AP and the Sriram Sagar in Telangana are key dams.

3. Krishna

Length: ~ 1,400 km. Source: Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra (Western Ghats). Flows through Maharashtra, Karnataka, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh; reaches the Bay near Machilipatnam, forming a delta that merges with the Godavari delta.

Major tributaries: Right bank — Koyna, Ghatprabha, Malaprabha, Tungabhadra (the largest). Left bank — Bhima, Musi (Hyderabad sits on it), Munneru. The Tungabhadra dam (Karnataka), the Almatti and Nagarjuna Sagar (Andhra) are key dams. Nagarjuna Sagar (Krishna, AP/Telangana) is the world's largest masonry dam.

4. Cauvery — see next section.

5. Smaller east-flowing rivers

Subarnarekha (Jharkhand/Odisha; only ~ 395 km but historically gold-bearing — hence the name "golden line"); Brahmani-Baitarani; Pennar (AP); Vaigai (TN — covered in Ch 19).

The Cauvery — Tamil Nadu's lifeblood.

The Cauvery (காவிரி) is the smallest of the four great east-flowing peninsular rivers, but the most culturally and economically important for Tamil Nadu. Length: ~ 800 km. Source: Talakaveri, Brahmagiri Hills, Kodagu district, Karnataka (Western Ghats), at an elevation of about 1,341 m.

The river flows southeast through Karnataka, enters Tamil Nadu after the Krishnaraja Sagar dam at Mysuru and Hogenakkal Falls, traverses the heart of Tamil Nadu through Erode–Tiruchirappalli–Thanjavur, and reaches the Bay of Bengal at Poompuhar (Kaveripattinam) in the Mayiladuthurai district.

Major tributaries

Right bank: Hemavati, Lakshmana Tirtha, Kabini (joins at Tirumakudal Narsipur), Suvarnavathi, Bhavani (joins at Bhavani, Erode — itself an important river fed from the Nilgiris), Noyyal (Coimbatore).

Left bank: Harangi, Shimsha, Arkavati. Smaller tributaries within TN: Amaravati (joins at Karur), Kollidam (the main distributary in the delta).

Delta and irrigation

At Tiruchirappalli, the Cauvery splits into two: the main Cauvery to the south and the Kollidam (Coleroon) to the north. Together they enclose the Srirangam island, the second-largest river island in India and seat of the great Ranganathaswamy temple. The delta — covering Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam, Mayiladuthurai, parts of Cuddalore and Pudukkottai — is the rice bowl of Tamil Nadu. The Grand Anicut (Kallanai) — built by the Chola king Karikalan in the 2nd century CE — is the oldest functioning dam in the world.

TAMIL NADU CONNECTION — KEY DAMS ON THE CAUVERY

• Krishnaraja Sagar (KRS) — Mandya, Karnataka. Built 1932 by Sir M. Visvesvaraya.
• Mettur Dam — Salem district, TN. Built 1934 on the Cauvery as it enters the plains. Creates Stanley Reservoir. One of the largest dams in India.
• Bhavanisagar Dam — on the Bhavani river, Erode.
• Amaravathi Dam — Tiruppur district.
• Kallanai (Grand Anicut) — Tiruchirappalli district. ~ 2,000 years old; still in service.

West-flowing rivers.

1. Narmada — the Reva

The fifth-longest river in India, longest west-flowing river. Length: ~ 1,312 km. Source: Amarkantak plateau, Maikal Range, Madhya Pradesh (~ 1,057 m elevation). Flows westward through a rift valley between the Vindhya (north) and Satpura (south), through MP, Maharashtra, and Gujarat. Empties into the Gulf of Khambhat near Bharuch through an estuary.

Famous features: Marble rocks at Bhedaghat (Jabalpur) — where the river cuts through marble for ~ 8 km; Dhuandhar Falls at the same location. The Sardar Sarovar Dam (Gujarat) is the world's second-largest concrete dam by volume; the Statue of Unity overlooks it.

2. Tapi (Tapti)

Second major west-flowing river. Length: ~ 724 km. Source: Multai, Betul district, MP (Satpura). Flows westward roughly parallel to the Narmada (south of it), through MP, Maharashtra, Gujarat; empties into the Arabian Sea near Surat (Gulf of Khambhat). Major tributary: Purna.

3. Other west-flowing rivers

  • Sabarmati — rises in Aravallis, flows through Gujarat; Ahmedabad sits on it; meets Gulf of Khambhat.
  • Mahi — rises in MP (Vindhyas), flows through Rajasthan and Gujarat; meets Gulf of Khambhat. Crosses the Tropic of Cancer twice.
  • Luni — rises in Aravalli; only river of Thar; never reaches the sea.
  • Sharavati — short Karnataka river; forms Jog Falls.
  • Periyar — Kerala's longest river; rises in Sivagiri Hills, flows past Idukki and Kochi backwaters.
  • Bharathappuzha (Nila) — second-longest Kerala river; flows through Palakkad gap area.

KEY FACT — INDIA'S FIRST HYDROELECTRIC PLANT

The hydroelectric station at Shivanasamudra Falls on the Cauvery, set up in 1902 to supply power to the Kolar gold fields, was the first hydroelectric plant in India and one of the earliest in Asia.

Major inter-state water disputes.

Cauvery dispute (Karnataka vs Tamil Nadu, with Kerala & Puducherry)

India's longest-running inter-state water dispute. Roots in colonial-era agreements (1892, 1924). The Cauvery Water Disputes Tribunal (CWDT) gave its final award in 2007. The Supreme Court modified allocations in 2018:

  • Tamil Nadu: 404.25 TMC (modified upward)
  • Karnataka: 284.75 TMC (modified upward by 14.75 TMC)
  • Kerala: 30 TMC
  • Puducherry: 7 TMC

The Cauvery Water Management Authority (CWMA) was constituted in 2018 to implement the award.

Krishna dispute (Maharashtra, Karnataka, AP, Telangana)

KWDT-I (Bachawat Commission, 1976) allocated water; KWDT-II (Brijesh Kumar, 2013) further apportioned the "available" water. The reorganisation of AP into AP & Telangana (2014) reopened sub-issues.

Mullaperiyar dispute (TN vs Kerala)

The 1886 lease between the British and the Maharaja of Travancore granted Tamil Nadu use of the Mullaperiyar Dam (built 1895 in Kerala's Idukki district) for 999 years. The dispute centres on dam safety and water level (Supreme Court allowed 142 ft against Kerala's demand for lower).

Mahanadi dispute (Odisha vs Chhattisgarh)

Odisha objects to upstream barrages in Chhattisgarh. A tribunal was constituted in 2018.

Ravi-Beas dispute (Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan)

Centred on the Sutlej-Yamuna Link canal (SYL). Long-running political flashpoint in Punjab.

QUICK RECAP

What this chapter said.

  • Peninsular rivers: shorter, seasonal, mature courses; mostly east-flowing because of the westward tilt of the plateau.
  • Major east-flowing rivers (N→S): Mahanadi (858), Godavari (1,465 — longest peninsular), Krishna (1,400), Cauvery (800).
  • Cauvery: Talakaveri (Karnataka) → Mettur → Tiruchirappalli (where it splits into Kaveri + Kollidam, around Srirangam island) → Poompuhar.
  • Kallanai (Grand Anicut) — world's oldest functioning dam; Kallanai/Mettur dams support Cauvery delta paddy.
  • West-flowing rivers: Narmada (1,312) and Tapi (724) — both in rift valleys; estuaries.
  • Cauvery dispute: SC 2018 — TN 404.25 TMC, Karnataka 284.75 TMC, Kerala 30, Puducherry 7.
  • Largest brackish lagoon — Chilika. Largest freshwater lake — Kolleru. Largest salt lake — Sambhar. Longest lake — Vembanad. Floating national park — Loktak (Keibul Lamjao).
  • Kunchikal Falls (Karnataka, 455 m) — tallest in India. Shivanasamudra — first hydroelectric station (1902).

Practice MCQs

CH 08 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The longest peninsular river of India is —
  1. Krishna
  2. Godavari
  3. Narmada
  4. Mahanadi
Reveal answer

B — Godavari (~ 1,465 km). Often called "Dakshin Ganga".

02The Cauvery originates at —
  1. Mahabaleshwar
  2. Trimbak
  3. Talakaveri
  4. Amarkantak
Reveal answer

C — Talakaveri on the Brahmagiri Hills, Kodagu, Karnataka.

03Kallanai (Grand Anicut) — the world's oldest functioning dam — was built on which river?
  1. Krishna
  2. Godavari
  3. Cauvery
  4. Vaigai
Reveal answer

C — Cauvery, by the Chola king Karikalan, ~ 2nd century CE.

04The Narmada flows in a rift valley between which two ranges?
  1. Aravalli & Vindhya
  2. Vindhya & Satpura
  3. Satpura & Western Ghats
  4. Eastern Ghats & Western Ghats
Reveal answer

B — Vindhya (north) & Satpura (south).

05The largest brackish-water lake in India is —
  1. Chilika
  2. Pulicat
  3. Vembanad
  4. Kolleru
Reveal answer

A — Chilika (Odisha). Pulicat is second-largest.

06The floating national park, Keibul Lamjao, is located on which lake?
  1. Wular
  2. Loktak
  3. Chilika
  4. Sambhar
Reveal answer

B — Loktak Lake (Manipur). Famous for its floating phumdi islands and the endangered sangai deer.

07India's first hydroelectric power station, set up in 1902, is located at —
  1. Mettur
  2. Jog Falls
  3. Shivanasamudra
  4. Dudhsagar
Reveal answer

C — Shivanasamudra Falls on the Cauvery in Karnataka. Powered the Kolar gold fields.

08The river that crosses the Tropic of Cancer twice is —
  1. Narmada
  2. Sabarmati
  3. Mahi
  4. Luni
Reveal answer

C — Mahi. Rises in MP, flows north into Rajasthan crossing the Tropic, then turns south through Gujarat crossing it again.

09The Mullaperiyar Dam is located in Kerala but operated by Tamil Nadu under a lease that runs for —
  1. 99 years
  2. 199 years
  3. 499 years
  4. 999 years
Reveal answer

D — 999 years. Signed in 1886 between the British and the Travancore Maharaja.

10The Srirangam temple sits on a river island formed by which river splitting into Kollidam and the main Cauvery?
  1. Vaigai
  2. Cauvery
  3. Tamiraparani
  4. Bhavani
Reveal answer

B — Cauvery. Srirangam is the second-largest river island in India.
