CHAPTER 13 / MINERALS & ENERGY

Minerals & Energy.

What the earth gives — and how India uses it to build steel, light bulbs, and the digital economy.

OBJECTIVES

  • Classify minerals (metallic, non-metallic, energy).
  • Locate India's major mineral belts and recall leading producers.
  • Distinguish coalfields (Gondwana vs Tertiary).
  • Identify oil basins and refineries.
  • Know nuclear power stations and renewable energy capacity.

Classification of minerals.

  • Metallic: iron, manganese, bauxite, copper, gold, lead, zinc, mica.
  • Non-metallic: limestone, dolomite, gypsum, phosphate, salt, silica, magnesite, asbestos.
  • Energy / mineral fuels: coal, petroleum, natural gas, uranium, thorium.
  • Atomic: uranium (Jaduguda), thorium (monazite sands of Kerala coast).

Metallic minerals.

Iron ore

India holds ~ 5–7% of global reserves. India is the 4th largest producer in the world. Two main types: hematite (most common) and magnetite.

  • Odisha (largest producer) — Sundargarh, Mayurbhanj, Keonjhar, Bonai.
  • Chhattisgarh — Bailadila, Dalli-Rajhara, Bastar (highest grade — exported via Visakhapatnam).
  • Karnataka — Bellary-Hospet, Kudremukh, Chitradurga.
  • Jharkhand — Singhbhum, Noamundi.
  • Goa — laterite-derived; lower grade.
  • Tamil Nadu — Salem (magnetite at Kanjamalai), Tiruchirappalli.

Manganese

Used in steel-making. India is among top 5 producers globally. Madhya Pradesh (largest, Balaghat), Maharashtra (Nagpur, Bhandara), Odisha, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh.

Bauxite (aluminium ore)

India 5th largest producer. Odisha (largest) — Koraput, Sundargarh; Jharkhand, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh. Tamil Nadu has small deposits in Yercaud (Salem) and Kolli Hills.

Copper

India is a small producer. Madhya Pradesh (Malanjkhand, largest), Rajasthan (Khetri, historic), Jharkhand (Singhbhum). Tamil Nadu: minor deposits at Mamandur and Singhbhum.

Mica

India is among the world's largest producers of sheet mica. Jharkhand (Koderma, Gaya — once "Mica capital of the world"), Andhra Pradesh (Nellore), Rajasthan (Bhilwara), Bihar.

Gold

India's two historic gold mines: Kolar Gold Fields (Karnataka) — closed in 2001 due to economic non-viability; Hutti (Karnataka) — still operational. Reserves also in Ramgiri (AP). India is a net importer.

Other metals

  • Lead-Zinc — Zawar & Rampura-Agucha (Rajasthan); Bhanrer (MP).
  • Chromite — Sukinda valley, Odisha (~ 95% of India's output).
  • Tungsten — Degana (Rajasthan).

Non-metallic minerals.

  • Limestone — used for cement & steel. Found in MP, Rajasthan, Andhra, Karnataka, Telangana, Tamil Nadu (Ariyalur, Tirunelveli).
  • Dolomite — Chhattisgarh, Odisha, MP.
  • Gypsum — Rajasthan (largest, Bikaner-Jodhpur), Tamil Nadu (Tiruchirappalli, Tirunelveli), Gujarat.
  • Magnesite — Tamil Nadu (Salem — largest in India), Uttarakhand, Karnataka.
  • Asbestos — Rajasthan, AP, Karnataka.
  • Phosphate — Rajasthan (Jhamarkotra), Uttarakhand.
TAMIL NADU CONNECTION

TN's mineral profile: magnesite (Salem — largest in India), lignite (Neyveli — largest lignite in India), limestone (Ariyalur, Tirunelveli — for cement), garnet sand & ilmenite (Manavalakurichi, Kanyakumari — rare-earth heavy mineral sands), gypsum (Tiruchirappalli, Tirunelveli), iron ore (Salem, Tiruchi), bauxite (Kolli Hills, Yercaud). Plus thorium-rich monazite from coastal sands.

Energy resources.

Total installed power capacity in India (2024): ~ 442 GW. Of this:

  • Coal/thermal — ~ 47% (largest share)
  • Renewables (solar+wind+small hydro+biomass) — ~ 28% combined
  • Large hydro — ~ 10.5%
  • Nuclear — ~ 1.7%
  • Gas, diesel — remainder

Coal — workhorse of Indian energy.

India is the 2nd largest producer and consumer of coal in the world. Coal accounts for ~ 73% of India's total electricity generation.

Two great coalfields

  • Gondwana coal (~ 250 million years old) — accounts for 99% of India's coal reserves. Found in: Damodar Valley (Jharia, Bokaro, Raniganj, Karanpura — Jharkhand & WB), Mahanadi Valley (Talcher in Odisha, Korba in Chhattisgarh), Son Valley (Singrauli in MP), Wardha Valley (Maharashtra), Godavari Valley (Telangana, AP).
  • Tertiary coal (~ 50 million years old) — found in NE India (Assam, Arunachal, Meghalaya, Nagaland). High sulphur; lower quality.

Leading coal-producing states

  1. Chhattisgarh (largest)
  2. Odisha
  3. Jharkhand
  4. Madhya Pradesh
  5. Telangana
  6. Maharashtra

Lignite (brown coal)

Lower-quality coal. Largest reserves at Neyveli (Tamil Nadu) — operated by NLC India. Other smaller deposits in Rajasthan (Barmer, Bikaner), Gujarat (Kachchh), Kerala, Puducherry.

Major refineries

India has 23 refineries with total refining capacity of ~ 257 MMTPA (2024). Major ones: Jamnagar (Reliance, Gujarat — world's largest refinery complex), Mathura, Koyali, Manali (Chennai — Indian Oil), Nagapattinam (CPCL), Vishakhapatnam, Cochin, Bina, Paradip, Numaligarh (Assam).

Nuclear energy.

India has 22 operational nuclear reactors at 8 sites with installed capacity of ~ 7.48 GW (2024). India follows a three-stage nuclear programme (Bhabha plan) using natural uranium → fast breeder reactors → thorium.

Atomic minerals

  • Uranium — Jaduguda, Bhatin, Narwapahar, Turamdih (Jharkhand); Tummalapalle (AP — world's largest known uranium reserve in a single mine, announced 2010).
  • Thorium — monazite sand on Kerala coast (Chavara), Tamil Nadu coast (Manavalakurichi), Odisha coast. India has ~ 25% of world's thorium reserves.

Renewable energy.

India's installed renewable capacity (2024): ~ 200 GW (target: 500 GW by 2030, including large hydro). India is the 4th largest in renewable capacity globally.

KEY FACT — TAMIL NADU IS A POWER LEADER

Tamil Nadu was the first state to cross 10 GW of wind and second state to cross 10 GW of solar. Bhadla Solar Park (Rajasthan, ~ 2.25 GW) is the largest park; Tamil Nadu's Kamuthi Solar Park (Ramanathapuram, 648 MW commissioned 2016) was the world's largest single-location solar park at the time of commissioning.
