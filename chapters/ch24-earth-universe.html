CHAPTER 24 / WORLD · EARTH & UNIVERSE

The Earth & the Universe.

From the Big Bang to GMT — the foundations of physical geography in space and time.

OBJECTIVES

  • Understand the universe, galaxy, and solar system structure.
  • Identify the planets and their key facts.
  • Define latitudes, longitudes, time zones.
  • Describe the Earth's rotation, revolution, seasons.
  • Understand the phases of the Moon and types of eclipses.

The Universe.

  • The currently accepted theory: the universe began ~ 13.8 billion years ago in the Big Bang.
  • Universe contains hundreds of billions of galaxies; each contains hundreds of billions of stars.
  • Our galaxy: Milky Way (called Akashganga in Sanskrit) — spiral galaxy, ~ 100,000 light years across, ~ 200-400 billion stars.
  • Sun is located in one of the spiral arms, the Orion Arm.
  • Light year: distance light travels in one Julian year ≈ 9.46 × 10¹² km.
  • Nearest star (other than the Sun): Proxima Centauri — 4.24 light years.
  • Nearest galaxy to Milky Way: Andromeda (M31) — ~ 2.5 million light years.

The Solar System.

Born ~ 4.6 billion years ago from a contracting cloud of gas and dust. Components:

  • 1 star: the Sun (99.86% of the system's mass).
  • 8 planets; planets ≥ 2006 IAU definition (Pluto demoted to "dwarf planet").
  • Asteroid belt (between Mars & Jupiter); Kuiper belt (beyond Neptune); Oort cloud (outer edge).
  • 5 recognised dwarf planets: Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, Ceres.
  • ~ 250 moons (natural satellites).

The Sun

  • ~ 4.6 billion years old (mid-life main sequence star, type G2V).
  • Diameter: ~ 1.39 million km — 109 times Earth's.
  • Surface (photosphere) temperature ~ 5,500°C; core ~ 15 million°C.
  • Composed mainly of hydrogen (~ 74%) and helium (~ 24%).
  • Light from Sun reaches Earth in ~ 8 minutes 20 seconds.

MNEMONIC — ORDER OF PLANETS

"My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos" — Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune.

Inner (terrestrial) vs Outer (Jovian)

  • Inner planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars — rocky, small, dense, fewer moons.
  • Outer planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune — gaseous (Jovian) / icy giants, much larger, many moons, rings.

The Earth.

  • Third planet from the Sun; fifth-largest by size.
  • Equatorial diameter: 12,756 km; polar diameter: 12,714 km (slight oblate spheroid).
  • Surface area: 510 million km² (~ 71% water, 29% land).
  • Mass: 5.972 × 10²⁴ kg.
  • Age: 4.54 billion years.
  • Tilted on its axis at 23.5° — the cause of seasons.
  • Atmosphere composition (lower): N₂ ~ 78%, O₂ ~ 21%, Ar ~ 0.93%, CO₂ ~ 0.04%, water vapour 0-4%.
  • Internal structure (layers): Crust, Mantle, Outer Core (liquid iron-nickel), Inner Core (solid iron).

Earth's motions.

Rotation

  • Earth rotates on its axis from west to east.
  • One sidereal day = 23h 56m 4s. One solar day = 24 hours.
  • Linear speed at equator ~ 1,670 km/h; at poles ~ 0.
  • Causes: day & night, rise/set of stars, Coriolis force.

Revolution

  • Earth orbits the Sun once in 365 days 5h 48m 46s.
  • Orbit slightly elliptical; closest to Sun in early January (perihelion ~ 147 million km), farthest in early July (aphelion ~ 152 million km).
  • Combined with the 23.5° tilt, revolution produces the seasons.

Solstices & Equinoxes

  • March equinox (~ March 21) — day and night equal everywhere.
  • June solstice (~ June 21) — longest day in Northern hemisphere (sun overhead at Tropic of Cancer).
  • September equinox (~ Sept 23) — equal day-night again.
  • December solstice (~ Dec 22) — shortest day in N hemisphere; sun overhead at Tropic of Capricorn.

Latitudes & longitudes.

Latitudes

  • Imaginary lines parallel to the Equator. Equator = 0°. North Pole = 90°N, South Pole = 90°S.
  • Important parallels:
    • Tropic of Cancer: 23.5° N
    • Tropic of Capricorn: 23.5° S
    • Arctic Circle: 66.5° N
    • Antarctic Circle: 66.5° S
  • One degree of latitude ≈ 111 km.

Longitudes (meridians)

  • Imaginary lines from pole to pole.
  • 0° = Prime Meridian, passing through Greenwich, London.
  • 180° meridian (mostly aligned with International Date Line, with deviations).
  • 360 meridians; 180 east, 180 west.

Time zones.

  • Earth rotates 360° in 24 hours → 15° = 1 hour; 1° = 4 minutes.
  • Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) / Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) is the reference.
  • India: UTC + 5:30, based on the Standard Meridian at 82°30′ E (Mirzapur).
  • International Date Line — runs roughly along 180° meridian (with deviations to avoid splitting countries). Crossing it east → west adds a day; crossing west → east subtracts a day.

The Moon.

  • Earth's only natural satellite; diameter 3,474 km (~ ¼ of Earth's).
  • Mean distance from Earth: 384,400 km.
  • Light from Moon reaches Earth in ~ 1.28 seconds.
  • Same side always faces Earth (tidally locked).
  • Sidereal month ~ 27.3 days; synodic month (cycle of phases) ~ 29.5 days.
  • Believed to have formed ~ 4.5 billion years ago when a Mars-sized body ("Theia") collided with the proto-Earth.
  • India's lunar missions: Chandrayaan-1 (2008, discovered water), Chandrayaan-2 (2019, partially successful), Chandrayaan-3 (2023, successful landing near south pole — making India the first country to soft-land there).

Eclipses.

  • Solar eclipse — Moon comes between Sun and Earth, blocking the Sun (partial / total / annular).
  • Lunar eclipse — Earth comes between Sun and Moon, casting its shadow on the Moon. Always at full moon.
  • Both happen 2-7 times a year.
  • Tides — caused by gravitational pull of Moon and Sun on Earth's oceans. Spring tides (highest) at new moon and full moon; neap tides (lowest) at quarter moons.
