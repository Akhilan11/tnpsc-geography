CHAPTER 18 / TAMIL NADU — FLAGSHIP

Tamil Nadu — Location & Districts.

The southernmost state of mainland India, the eleventh largest, the sixth most populous — and the most exam-tested in the TNPSC syllabus.

OBJECTIVES

  • State Tamil Nadu's location, latitudinal & longitudinal extent, and area.
  • Identify all border states and water bodies.
  • Memorise state symbols and capital.
  • Know the 38 districts — with formation year and the parent district where applicable.
  • Understand the regional divisions used in TN administration.

Tamil Nadu (தமிழ்நாடு) is among the most exam-relevant chapters in the entire TNPSC syllabus. This part of the book is the heart of your preparation. Every Tamil Nadu chapter, including this one, is written assuming you will be tested directly on each fact. Read it slowly, return often, and learn the maps.

Location & extent.

Tamil Nadu lies on the south-east of the Indian peninsula. Its absolute extent (mainland):

  • Latitudinal: 8° 05′ N to 13° 35′ N
  • Longitudinal: 76° 15′ E to 80° 20′ E
  • Tropic of Cancer does not pass through TN; the entire state lies south of it.
  • Capital: Chennai (formerly Madras).

Boundaries.

  • North: Andhra Pradesh & Karnataka
  • West: Kerala
  • East: Bay of Bengal
  • South: Gulf of Mannar, Palk Strait (Sri Lanka across the water)
  • Puducherry — Union Territory enclaves within TN: Puducherry & Karaikal (TN coast); Yanam (AP) and Mahe (Kerala) are non-TN parts of Puducherry UT.

Area & rank.

  • Total area: 1,30,058 km²
  • Approximately 4% of India.
  • 11th largest state by area.
  • Coastline: 1,076 km — 2nd longest after Gujarat.
  • Population (Census 2011): 7.21 crore — 6th most populous state.
  • Density 555/km²; sex ratio 996; literacy 80.1%; urban 48.4% (highest among major states).

Administration.

Tamil Nadu was carved out of Madras State on the basis of language (after the States Reorganisation Act, 1956, the Tamil-speaking regions). Renamed from Madras State to Tamil Nadu in 14 January 1969. Capital: Chennai. Currently has:

  • 38 districts
  • 87 divisions (revenue divisions)
  • 310 taluks (recent additions)
  • 1,127 firkas
  • 16,564 revenue villages
  • 21 corporations, 138 municipalities, 490 town panchayats, 12,524 village panchayats (approximate, 2024)

KEY FACT — DISTRICTS BY SIZE

Largest district by area: Salem (~ 5,205 km²).
Smallest district by area: Chennai (~ 426 km²).
Most populous district: Chennai (~ 46 lakh, Census 2011); Kanchipuram is largest by population if Chennai's metropolitan area is excluded.
Least populated district: Perambalur (~ 5.6 lakh).
Highest literacy: Kanyakumari (~ 91.7%).
Lowest literacy: Dharmapuri (~ 68.8%).
Highest sex ratio: Nilgiris.
Lowest sex ratio: Dharmapuri / Salem.

Regional and revenue divisions.

TN is administratively organised into 4 revenue regions headed by Revenue Divisional Commissioners:

  1. Chennai region — Chennai, Tiruvallur, Kanchipuram, Chengalpattu, Vellore, Ranipet, Tirupattur, Tiruvannamalai, Krishnagiri, Dharmapuri.
  2. Coimbatore region — Coimbatore, Tiruppur, Erode, Namakkal, Salem, Nilgiris.
  3. Tiruchirappalli region — Tiruchi, Karur, Perambalur, Ariyalur, Cuddalore, Villupuram, Kallakurichi, Pudukkottai, Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam, Mayiladuthurai.
  4. Madurai region — Madurai, Dindigul, Theni, Sivagangai, Ramanathapuram, Virudhunagar, Tirunelveli, Tenkasi, Thoothukudi, Kanyakumari.

Traditional cultural regions of TN

  • Tondaimandalam — north (Chennai, Tiruvallur, Kanchipuram, Vellore, Tiruvannamalai).
  • Cholamandalam — central east (Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam, Tiruchirappalli).
  • Pandyamandalam — south (Madurai, Tirunelveli, Ramanathapuram, Thoothukudi).
  • Kongumandalam — west (Coimbatore, Erode, Tiruppur, Salem, Namakkal).
  • Cheramandalam — south-west (Kanyakumari and parts of Western Ghats border).

Practice MCQs

CH 18 · 10 QUESTIONS
01Tamil Nadu's coastline (length, mainland) is —
  1. 906 km
  2. 1,076 km
  3. 1,214 km
  4. 1,440 km
Reveal answer

B — 1,076 km — 2nd longest after Gujarat (1,214 km).

02The state of Madras was renamed Tamil Nadu in —
  1. 1956
  2. 1963
  3. 1969
  4. 1971
Reveal answer

C — 14 January 1969.

03The state animal of Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Tiger
  2. Indian Elephant
  3. Nilgiri Tahr
  4. Blackbuck
Reveal answer

C — Nilgiri Tahr (வரையாடு).

04The largest district by area in Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Coimbatore
  2. Salem
  3. Vellore
  4. Tirunelveli
Reveal answer

B — Salem (~ 5,205 km²).

05Tamil Nadu shares its longest land border with —
  1. Karnataka
  2. Andhra Pradesh
  3. Kerala
  4. Puducherry
Reveal answer

A — Karnataka (~ northern boundary) — but TN's longest state border is contested between AP and Karnataka by length depending on definition. The TNPSC-accepted answer is Karnataka.

06The state flower of Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Lotus
  2. Hibiscus
  3. Glory Lily (Gloriosa)
  4. Marigold
Reveal answer

C — Glory Lily (காந்தள்).

07The district with the highest literacy rate in Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Chennai
  2. Coimbatore
  3. Kanyakumari
  4. Nilgiris
Reveal answer

C — Kanyakumari (~ 91.7%, Census 2011).

08The total number of districts in Tamil Nadu (as of 2024) is —
  1. 32
  2. 36
  3. 38
  4. 40
Reveal answer

C — 38 districts.

09The traditional "Cholamandalam" region corresponds to —
  1. North TN (Chennai region)
  2. Central-eastern TN (Cauvery delta)
  3. South TN (Madurai region)
  4. West TN (Coimbatore region)
Reveal answer

B — Central-eastern TN — the Cauvery delta heartland.

10Tamil Nadu has how many revenue regions?
  1. 3
  2. 4
  3. 5
  4. 6
Reveal answer

B — 4 revenue regions: Chennai, Coimbatore, Tiruchirappalli, Madurai.
