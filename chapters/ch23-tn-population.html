CHAPTER 23 / TAMIL NADU — PEOPLE & TOURISM

TN — Population & Tourism.

India's 6th most populous state, most urbanised among the big states, and the country's leading tourist destination by visitor numbers.

OBJECTIVES

  • Recall TN's demographic profile (Census 2011 and beyond).
  • Locate the metropolitan areas.
  • Understand TN's road, rail, port and airport network.
  • Memorise the temple circuits, hill stations, beaches, and UNESCO sites of TN.

Demography of Tamil Nadu.

  • Population (Census 2011): 7,21,47,030 (~ 7.21 crore).
  • National rank: 6th.
  • Decadal growth (2001-11): 15.6% (below national 17.7%).
  • Density: 555/km².
  • Sex ratio: 996 — among the best.
  • Child (0-6) sex ratio: 943.
  • Literacy: 80.1% (male 86.8%, female 73.4%).
  • Urban share: 48.4% — highest among major states.
  • SC population: 20.0% (high concentration).
  • ST population: 1.1% (low — mainly Nilgiris tribes — Toda, Kota, Irula, Paniyan, Kurumba).
  • Hindu majority: ~ 88%; Christians ~ 6%; Muslims ~ 6%.
  • TFR: ~ 1.8 (below replacement) per NFHS-5.

Literacy & Human Development.

TN's HDI is among the highest in India. Female literacy in TN is among the best for any large Indian state. Kanyakumari is the most literate district (~ 92%). The TN free-mid-day-meal scheme (launched by Kamaraj in 1956, expanded by MGR 1982) is widely credited with school retention rates.

Transport network of Tamil Nadu.

Roads

  • Total road length: ~ 2.7 lakh km — among India's densest road networks.
  • NH-44 (Srinagar-Kanyakumari) terminates in TN.
  • NH-48 (formerly NH-4) — Chennai-Bengaluru-Mumbai.
  • NH-32 (formerly NH-45) — Chennai-Tiruchi-Madurai-Kanyakumari (East coast).
  • NH-83 — Chennai outer ring.
  • SH network is extensive; State Transport Corporation (TNSTC) operates one of India's largest bus fleets.

Railways

  • Southern Railway zone headquartered at Chennai (Park Town).
  • Chennai Central (Puratchi Thalaivar Dr. M. G. Ramachandran Central) — major terminus; among India's busiest stations.
  • Chennai Egmore — second major terminus.
  • Other major junctions: Coimbatore, Madurai, Tiruchirappalli, Salem, Erode, Tirunelveli, Katpadi, Villupuram.
  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway — UNESCO World Heritage; Mettupalayam → Coonoor → Ooty.
  • Chennai Metro — operational since 2015; expanding Phase 2 underway.

Ports

  • Major: Chennai, Ennore (Kamarajar), V. O. Chidambaranar (Tuticorin).
  • Other: Nagapattinam, Karaikal (Pondicherry UT), Cuddalore, Colachel (Vizhinjam alternative — proposed transshipment).

Airports

  • International: Chennai (Anna), Coimbatore, Madurai, Tiruchirappalli, Tuticorin.
  • Domestic: Salem, Tanjavur (Air Force, civil enclave), Hosur (under development).

Tourism — TN is India's #1 by visitors.

Tamil Nadu has been the most-visited Indian state by tourist arrivals for over a decade (~ 30%+ of all domestic tourist visits in India in recent years). The base is religious tourism, supplemented by heritage, hill stations, beaches, and wellness.

Major temple cities

  • Madurai — Meenakshi Amman Temple (one of Dravidian architecture's greatest).
  • Tiruchirappalli — Rockfort Temple (Uchipillaiyar), Srirangam Ranganathaswamy Temple (largest functioning Hindu temple in the world by area).
  • Thanjavur — Brihadeeswarar Temple (UNESCO, Chola, ~ 1010 CE).
  • Rameswaram — Ramanathaswamy Temple; one of 12 Jyotirlingas; Char Dham.
  • Tiruvannamalai — Annamalaiyar Temple, Arunachala Hill.
  • Chidambaram — Nataraja Temple.
  • Kanchipuram — Ekambareswarar, Kailasanathar, Kamakshi; "city of 1,000 temples".
  • Velankanni — Basilica of Our Lady of Health (largest Marian shrine in S Asia).
  • Nagore — Nagore Dargah (Sufi shrine).
  • Palani — Murugan temple (one of six abodes of Murugan).
  • Thiruchendur, Swamimalai, Tiruparankunram, Pazhamudircholai — the six abodes of Murugan ("Arupadai Veedu").

Hill stations

  • Udhagamandalam (Ooty) — Nilgiris. "Queen of Hill Stations".
  • Kodaikanal — Palani Hills. "Princess of Hill Stations".
  • Yercaud — Shevaroy Hills, Salem.
  • Coonoor — Nilgiris.
  • Yelagiri — Tirupattur.
  • Kotagiri — Nilgiris.
  • Valparai — Anaimalai Hills, Coimbatore.
  • Megamalai — Theni.
  • Topslip — Anaimalai region.

Beaches

  • Marina Beach (Chennai) — one of the longest urban beaches in the world.
  • Elliot's Beach (Besant Nagar) — Chennai.
  • Mahabalipuram, Tiger Cave Beach.
  • Rameswaram, Dhanushkodi.
  • Kanyakumari — sunrise/sunset over three seas.
  • Kovalam (TN) — Chengalpattu.
  • Velankanni, Poompuhar, Nagapattinam, Karaikal.
  • Tiruchendur, Manapad — Thoothukudi.

Heritage & Wildlife

  • Mahabalipuram — UNESCO; Pallava shore temples; Five Rathas; Krishna's Butter Ball.
  • Brihadeeswarar Temple (Thanjavur), Airavatesvara (Darasuram), Gangaikondacholapuram — three Chola temples, UNESCO ("Great Living Chola Temples").
  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway — UNESCO.
  • Western Ghats — UNESCO Natural site (parts in TN).
  • Mudumalai, Anaimalai, Sathyamangalam, Kalakkad-Mundanthurai, Mukurthi — wildlife parks.

UNESCO World Heritage Sites in (or partly in) Tamil Nadu.

  1. Mahabalipuram (Mamallapuram) — Group of Monuments (1984).
  2. Great Living Chola Temples — Brihadeeswarar (Thanjavur), Brihadeeswarar (Gangaikondacholapuram), Airavateswara (Darasuram) — (1987 + 2004 additions).
  3. Nilgiri Mountain Railway — part of "Mountain Railways of India" (1999/2005).
  4. Western Ghats — Natural site (2012) — TN portions: Nilgiris, Anaimalai, Periyar (boundary), Megamalai, Agasthyamalai.

Tourism circuits.

  • Buddhist Circuit — Kanchipuram, Nagapattinam.
  • Sufi-Christian Circuit — Nagore, Velankanni, Vailankanni.
  • Murugan Arupadai Veedu — Six Abodes of Murugan (Palani, Tiruchendur, Pazhamudircholai, Swamimalai, Tiruparankunram, Pazhani).
  • Pancha Bhoota Stalam — five elemental Shiva temples — Chidambaram (sky), Tiruvanaikaval/Jambukeswarar (water), Tiruvannamalai (fire), Kanchipuram (earth), Kalahasti (wind, in AP).
  • Navagraha Temples — nine planetary temples in Cauvery delta.
  • Chola Heritage Circuit — Thanjavur, Gangaikondacholapuram, Darasuram.
  • Western Ghats Trail — Ooty, Coonoor, Kodaikanal, Valparai, Top Slip, Anaimalai.
  • Pondicherry Heritage (adjacent UT) — French quarter, Auroville.
TAMIL NADU CONNECTION — TOURISM SCALE

By India Tourism statistics, Tamil Nadu has consistently ranked No. 1 in domestic tourist visits (often > 350 million annually in recent years). The combination of temple, hill, heritage, and medical tourism (Chennai is South Asia's medical-tourism capital) makes TN a uniquely diverse destination.

Practice MCQs

CH 23 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The largest functioning Hindu temple in the world by area is —
  1. Meenakshi Amman (Madurai)
  2. Brihadeeswarar (Thanjavur)
  3. Ranganathaswamy (Srirangam)
  4. Kanchi Ekambareswarar
Reveal answer

C — Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, Srirangam.

02The Brihadeeswarar Temple at Thanjavur (UNESCO) was built by —
  1. Rajendra Chola I
  2. Rajaraja Chola I
  3. Vikrama Chola
  4. Kulottunga I
Reveal answer

B — Rajaraja Chola I (consecrated c. 1010 CE).

03"Queen of Hill Stations" in Tamil Nadu is —
  1. Kodaikanal
  2. Ooty (Udhagamandalam)
  3. Yercaud
  4. Yelagiri
Reveal answer

B — Ooty. Kodaikanal is "Princess".

04Tamil Nadu's most literate district is —
  1. Chennai
  2. Coimbatore
  3. Kanyakumari
  4. Nilgiris
Reveal answer

C — Kanyakumari (~ 91.75%, Census 2011).

05The Nilgiri Mountain Railway runs from —
  1. Coimbatore to Ooty
  2. Mettupalayam to Ooty
  3. Coonoor to Ooty only
  4. Mettupalayam to Coonoor only
Reveal answer

B — Mettupalayam to Ooty, via Coonoor.

06The temple town that is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Shiva (in TN) is —
  1. Madurai
  2. Rameswaram
  3. Tiruvannamalai
  4. Chidambaram
Reveal answer

B — Rameswaram (Ramanathaswamy temple).

07Velankanni is famous for —
  1. Murugan temple
  2. Marian Catholic shrine
  3. Sufi dargah
  4. Buddhist stupa
Reveal answer

B — Basilica of Our Lady of Good Health, Velankanni — largest Marian shrine in South Asia.

08Tamil Nadu has how many million-plus urban agglomerations (Census 2011)?
  1. 7
  2. 9
  3. 11
  4. 13
Reveal answer

C — 11 (Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Tiruchi, Tiruppur, Salem, Erode, Vellore, Thoothukudi, Tirunelveli, Dindigul — varies slightly by source).

09Mahabalipuram's shore temples were built during the —
  1. Chola period
  2. Chera period
  3. Pallava period
  4. Pandya period
Reveal answer

C — Pallava period (7th-8th centuries CE, Mahendravarman I & Narasimhavarman I).

10The "Pancha Bhoota Stalam" associated with the "earth" element is at —
  1. Chidambaram
  2. Tiruvanaikaval
  3. Tiruvannamalai
  4. Kanchipuram (Ekambareswarar)
Reveal answer

D — Kanchipuram (Ekambareswarar) — earth. Chidambaram = sky; Tiruvanaikaval = water; Tiruvannamalai = fire; Sri Kalahasti (AP) = wind.
