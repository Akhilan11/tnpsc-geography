CHAPTER 21 / TAMIL NADU — AGRICULTURE

TN — Agriculture & Irrigation.

From the paddy delta of the Cauvery to the cardamom slopes of the Western Ghats — the agricultural mosaic of Tamil Nadu.

OBJECTIVES

  • Recall TN's agricultural profile and contribution to India.
  • Identify the 7 agro-climatic zones of TN.
  • Memorise leading crops, top-producing districts, and seasonal calendars.
  • Understand TN's irrigation systems — tanks, canals, wells.
  • Know major schemes for TN farmers.

Agricultural overview.

  • Net sown area in TN: ~ 52 lakh hectares (~ 40% of geographical area).
  • Gross sown area: ~ 60 lakh hectares.
  • Cropping intensity: ~ 117% (low — needs irrigation development).
  • Workforce in agriculture: ~ 39% (TN is less agrarian than the Indian average of ~ 45%).
  • TN ranks among top states in: banana (1st), turmeric (1st), tapioca (1st), sugarcane productivity (1st), groundnut (top), flowers (1st).

Paddy — TN's principal food grain.

Tamil Nadu is among India's top rice producers — typically ranks 6th-8th in absolute production but high in productivity per hectare. TN's paddy is grown in three seasons (already met in Ch 12):

  • Sornavari (Kuruvai) — April-September. Short duration; sown with SW monsoon water; rapid. Largely confined to Cauvery delta.
  • Samba — August-January. The main paddy season; sown with NE monsoon water; medium-long duration.
  • Navarai / Thaladi — January-April. Rabi-like; tail-end of NE monsoon; in delta and well-irrigated areas.

Top paddy districts: Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam, Mayiladuthurai, Cuddalore, Villupuram, Tiruvannamalai, Tiruchi, Ariyalur, Kanchipuram, Salem.

Famous TN rice varieties

  • Ponni — high-yield variety popular in TN markets.
  • Mappillai samba — traditional indigenous variety; "wedding rice".
  • Kichili samba, Karuppu kavuni, Thooyamalli — heirloom varieties.
  • HYV: ADT series, CO series (developed by TNAU, Coimbatore).

Millets & pulses.

Drought-resilient and growing in TN as climate adaptation. Top millet districts: Salem, Dharmapuri, Krishnagiri, Tiruvannamalai, Vellore, Namakkal. Ragi, cumbu (pearl millet), thinai (foxtail), kuthiraivali (barnyard), kelvaragu, samai.

Pulses: Black gram (urad), green gram (moong), red gram (tur), horse gram. Largely in Vellore, Tiruvannamalai, Krishnagiri, Salem, Madurai, Sivagangai.

Sugarcane.

TN is among the most productive sugarcane states in India (highest productivity per hectare on average). Major districts: Cuddalore, Villupuram, Tiruvannamalai, Vellore, Erode, Coimbatore, Salem, Tiruchi, Theni, Madurai. Sugar mills: Pugalur, Annamalai, Subramaniya Siva.

Cotton.

Mostly in: Perambalur, Salem, Erode, Coimbatore, Theni, Virudhunagar, Tirunelveli, Thoothukudi. TN has historically been a cotton-deficit state (consuming more for its textile industry than it grows). Three varieties: short (Asian), medium, long (American). TNAU has developed several improved varieties.

Plantation crops.

Tea

TN is India's 3rd largest tea producer (after Assam & WB). Mainly in: Nilgiris (largest), Anaimalai (Coimbatore), Tirunelveli (Manjolai). Famous estate: Ooty/Coonoor tea.

Coffee

Mostly in: Nilgiris, Yercaud (Shevaroy Hills, Salem), Pulney Hills (Dindigul), Anaimalai. Coffee Board headquartered in Bengaluru.

Rubber

Mostly in: Kanyakumari, Nilgiris, Coimbatore.

Cardamom, Pepper, Cloves, Nutmeg

Mostly in Western Ghats: Theni (Megamalai), Tirunelveli, Anaimalai region, Kanyakumari, Dindigul.

Turmeric

Tamil Nadu (with Telangana) is among the largest turmeric producers in India. Erode is the most famous turmeric trading centre — "Turmeric City of India". Got a GI tag for Erode Manjal.

Horticulture.

  • Banana — TN is the largest banana producer in India. Major districts: Theni, Tiruchi, Erode, Coimbatore, Kanyakumari. Famous varieties: Poovan, Rasthali, Sirumalai banana (GI tag), Virupakshi.
  • Mango — Krishnagiri (largest), Dharmapuri, Vellore, Tirunelveli. Famous: Salem Mallika, Rumani.
  • Coconut — Coimbatore, Tiruppur, Pollachi, Kanyakumari, Theni, Dindigul. TN is among top 3 producers.
  • Cashew — Pudukkottai, Cuddalore, Sivagangai.
  • Tapioca (cassava) — TN is the largest producer in India. Salem (largest producer), Namakkal, Erode, Villupuram. Used in starch & sago industries.
  • Flowers — TN is the largest flower producer in India (jasmine, rose, marigold, chrysanthemum). Hosur is the centre for cut flowers (rose); Madurai for jasmine ("Madurai Malli" — GI tag).
  • Vegetables — diverse; major: tomato (Krishnagiri, Dharmapuri), onion, beans, drumstick.

Irrigation in Tamil Nadu.

TN's irrigation is unique: it relies heavily on tanks — small reservoirs accumulated over centuries — alongside canals (mostly Cauvery system) and wells. TN has more than 40,000 tanks; system-tanks (fed by river) and rain-fed tanks.

Sources of irrigation in TN (approximate)

  • Wells (including borewells) — ~ 55% of irrigated area (the dominant source).
  • Canals — ~ 25%, mostly Cauvery system.
  • Tanks — ~ 18%; traditionally much higher (was 35% in 1960s).
  • Others: lift, drip, sprinkler.

Major irrigation projects

  • Cauvery system — Mettur Dam & Stanley Reservoir, Grand Anicut, Lower Anicut, Kollidam regulators.
  • Periyar-Vaigai system — TN draws water from the Periyar river in Kerala via the Mullaperiyar Dam, then distributes via the Vaigai system.
  • Bhavanisagar Project — Bhavani, Erode; irrigates Lower Bhavani.
  • Amaravathi Project — Tiruppur.
  • Parambikulam Aliyar Project (PAP) — Inter-state with Kerala, irrigates Coimbatore-Tiruppur.
  • Vaigai Dam — Theni; irrigates Madurai region.
  • Sathanur Dam — Pennaiyar; Tiruvannamalai.
  • Manimuthar & Papanasam — Tamiraparani; Tirunelveli.
  • Krishnagiri Dam — Thenpennai.
  • Veeranam Tank — 8th century Chola tank; supplies Chennai.
TAMIL NADU CONNECTION — KUDIMARAMATHU

"Kudimaramathu" is a centuries-old Tamil tradition where farmers collectively maintain irrigation tanks and channels. The TN government revived it as a formal scheme in 2017, restoring 100s of tanks. This is a high-frequency TNPSC topic.

Major TN agricultural schemes & institutions.

  • Tamil Nadu Agricultural University (TNAU) — Coimbatore, established 1971. Has developed all the ADT, CO, and TKM rice varieties.
  • TANUVAS — Tamil Nadu Veterinary & Animal Sciences University, Chennai.
  • TN Fisheries University — Nagapattinam.
  • Kalaignar All-Village Integrated Agricultural Development Programme.
  • Uzhavar Sandhai (Farmers Market) — direct producer-consumer market across the state.
  • Free electricity for farmers — long-standing TN policy.
  • Crop insurance, soil health cards, micro-irrigation subsidy.
  • Kalaignar Magalir Urimai Thogai — women's monthly income scheme (2023) — has indirect implications for women in farming.
QUICK RECAP

What this chapter said.

  • 7 agro-climatic zones in TN: NE, NW, Western (Kongu), Cauvery Delta, Southern, High Rainfall (Kanyakumari), Hilly.
  • TN is the largest producer of: banana, tapioca, flowers, turmeric (and one of top in sugarcane productivity).
  • Paddy seasons: Sornavari (kuruvai), Samba (main), Navarai/Thaladi (rabi). Cauvery delta = rice bowl.
  • Erode = "Turmeric City" with GI tag. Madurai Malli (jasmine) has GI.
  • Irrigation: wells dominant (55%), canals 25%, tanks 18%. TN has 40,000+ tanks.
  • Major projects: Mettur, Bhavanisagar, Amaravathi, PAP, Vaigai, Sathanur, Papanasam, Mullaperiyar.
  • TNAU (1971, Coimbatore) — primary agricultural research body.
  • Kudimaramathu — traditional farmer-led tank maintenance, revived 2017.

Practice MCQs

CH 21 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The "rice bowl of Tamil Nadu" is —
  1. Vaigai plain
  2. Cauvery delta
  3. Palar plain
  4. Tamiraparani basin
Reveal answer

B — Cauvery delta (Thanjavur, Tiruvarur, Nagapattinam, Mayiladuthurai).

02The "Turmeric City of India" is —
  1. Erode
  2. Coimbatore
  3. Salem
  4. Theni
Reveal answer

A — Erode. Erode Manjal has GI tag.

03India's largest banana producer is —
  1. Maharashtra
  2. Andhra Pradesh
  3. Tamil Nadu
  4. Karnataka
Reveal answer

C — Tamil Nadu.

04"Madurai Malli" (GI-tagged jasmine) and the cut-rose hub of TN at Hosur both belong to which category?
  1. Cereals
  2. Flowers
  3. Plantation crops
  4. Pulses
Reveal answer

B — Flowers. TN is India's largest flower producer.

05The main paddy season of Tamil Nadu (sown with NE monsoon water) is —
  1. Sornavari
  2. Samba
  3. Navarai
  4. Thaladi
Reveal answer

B — Samba (Aug-Jan).

06The Tamil Nadu Agricultural University (TNAU) is located at —
  1. Chennai
  2. Madurai
  3. Coimbatore
  4. Tiruchirappalli
Reveal answer

C — Coimbatore (established 1971).

07The dominant source of irrigation in Tamil Nadu (by area irrigated) is —
  1. Canals
  2. Tanks
  3. Wells & borewells
  4. Lift & drip
Reveal answer

C — Wells (~ 55%). Canals next (~ 25%), then tanks (~ 18%).

08The Parambikulam-Aliyar Project (PAP) is an inter-state project between —
  1. TN & Karnataka
  2. TN & Kerala
  3. TN & AP
  4. TN & Pondicherry
Reveal answer

B — TN & Kerala.

09"Kudimaramathu" refers to —
  1. Free electricity to farmers
  2. Traditional tank maintenance by farmers
  3. A rice variety
  4. A revenue settlement system
Reveal answer

B — Traditional tank maintenance by farmers; revived as a formal scheme in 2017.

10India's largest producer of tapioca is —
  1. Andhra Pradesh
  2. Tamil Nadu (Salem belt)
  3. Kerala
  4. Karnataka
Reveal answer

B — Tamil Nadu; Salem is the centre. Used for starch and sago.
