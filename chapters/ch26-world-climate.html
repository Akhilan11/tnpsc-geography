CHAPTER 26 / WORLD · CLIMATE & RESOURCES

World Climate & Resources.

How temperature and rainfall pattern the planet — and where its people, food, and minerals are found.

OBJECTIVES

  • Identify the major climate zones using the Koppen system.
  • Recall world population trends and most populous cities.
  • Know the world's leading producers and consumers of key crops and minerals.

World population.

  • World population (2024): ~ 8.1 billion (UN estimate).
  • India: ~ 1.43 billion (largest).
  • China: ~ 1.42 billion (2nd).
  • USA: ~ 340 million (3rd).
  • Indonesia, Pakistan, Nigeria, Brazil, Bangladesh — round out the top 8-10.
  • UN projects world population to peak around 10.4 billion in the 2080s.
  • Most densely populated countries: Monaco, Singapore, Bangladesh, Bahrain.
  • Most populous continent: Asia (~ 60%).

World's largest urban agglomerations (UN 2024).

QUICK RECAP

What this chapter said.

  • Koppen classification: A (tropical), B (dry), C (temperate), D (continental), E (polar), H (highland).
  • World population: ~ 8.1 billion (2024); India largest, China 2nd.
  • Top crops/India: Rice (2nd), Wheat (2nd), Cotton (1st), Tea (2nd), Sugarcane (2nd), Milk (1st), Jute (1st).
  • Top minerals: Iron ore (Australia 1st, India 4th), Bauxite (Australia), Copper (Chile), Gold (China), Lithium (Australia).
  • Top oil producer: USA. Top coal producer: China.
  • Largest urban agglomeration (2024): Tokyo (~ 37 million).

Practice MCQs

CH 26 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The largest producer of cotton in the world is —
  1. USA
  2. China
  3. India
  4. Brazil
Reveal answer

C — India.

02The largest urban agglomeration in the world (2024) is —
  1. Delhi
  2. Tokyo
  3. Shanghai
  4. New York
Reveal answer

B — Tokyo (~ 37 million). Delhi is 2nd.

03The "Lithium Triangle" comprises which three countries?
  1. Australia, Chile, Argentina
  2. Bolivia, Chile, Argentina
  3. Brazil, Bolivia, Peru
  4. USA, Canada, Mexico
Reveal answer

B — Bolivia, Chile, Argentina. Together they hold ~ 60% of world lithium reserves.

04The Mediterranean climate is characterised by —
  1. Cold dry summer, hot wet winter
  2. Warm dry summer, mild wet winter
  3. Year-round rainfall
  4. Cold year round
Reveal answer

B — Warm dry summer, mild wet winter. Found in Mediterranean basin, California, Cape, central Chile, SW Australia.

05The world's largest coffee producer is —
  1. Colombia
  2. Vietnam
  3. Brazil
  4. Ethiopia
Reveal answer

C — Brazil.

06The largest producer of uranium in the world is —
  1. Canada
  2. Australia
  3. Kazakhstan
  4. Russia
Reveal answer

C — Kazakhstan (~ 40% of world).

07"BWh" in Koppen's classification refers to —
  1. Tropical wet
  2. Hot desert
  3. Mediterranean
  4. Polar tundra
Reveal answer

B — Hot desert. "B" = dry, "W" = desert, "h" = hot.

08India is the world's largest producer of —
  1. Wheat
  2. Rice
  3. Jute
  4. Maize
Reveal answer

C — Jute. India is 2nd in rice & wheat; jute is a clear 1st.

09The world's largest producer of bauxite is —
  1. China
  2. Australia
  3. Guinea
  4. India
Reveal answer

B — Australia.

10World population (2024) is approximately —
  1. 6.5 billion
  2. 7.2 billion
  3. 8.1 billion
  4. 9.5 billion
Reveal answer

C — ~ 8.1 billion (UN World Population Prospects 2024).
