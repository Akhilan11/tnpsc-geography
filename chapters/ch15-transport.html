CHAPTER 15 / TRANSPORT & COMMUNICATION

Transport & Communication.

Roads, rails, ships, planes, pipelines, fibre — the arteries that move India's people, goods, and information.

OBJECTIVES

  • Classify Indian roads and key NHs.
  • Understand the structure of Indian Railways — zones, gauges, key trains.
  • Recall major inland waterways (NWs) and the 12 major ports.
  • Identify airports and aviation regulators.
  • Map major pipelines and communication infrastructure.

Roads — the longest network.

India has the 2nd largest road network in the world (after the USA) — total length ~ 63.7 lakh km (2024). Roads carry ~ 87% of passenger traffic and ~ 60% of freight in India.

Classification of roads

  1. National Highways (NH) — ~ 1,46,000 km, ~ 2.3% of road length but ~ 40% of total road traffic. Maintained by NHAI / MoRTH.
  2. State Highways (SH) — connect state capitals with district hqs.
  3. District Roads — major (MDR) and other district roads (ODR).
  4. Rural roads — Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY) since 2000.
  5. Border Roads — built and maintained by Border Roads Organisation (BRO).

Key NHs and expressways

  • NH-44 — longest NH in India (~ 3,745 km). Srinagar → Kanyakumari. Earlier known as NH-7.
  • Golden Quadrilateral — connects Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata (~ 5,846 km). Completed mid-2000s.
  • North-South corridor — Srinagar to Kanyakumari.
  • East-West corridor — Porbandar (Gujarat) to Silchar (Assam).
  • Delhi-Mumbai Expressway — India's longest expressway (~ 1,386 km), opened in phases from 2023.
  • Yamuna Expressway, Mumbai-Pune Expressway, Eastern & Western Peripheral Expressways.

Indian Railways.

Indian Railways is the world's 4th largest rail network (after USA, China, Russia) and one of the world's largest employers. Total route length: ~ 68,584 km (2024); ~ 13,500 passenger trains daily; ~ 23 million passengers a day.

Zones

Indian Railways is organised into 18 zones:

  1. Northern (Delhi)
  2. North Western (Jaipur)
  3. North Eastern (Gorakhpur)
  4. Northeast Frontier (Maligaon, Guwahati)
  5. North Central (Prayagraj)
  6. Eastern (Kolkata)
  7. East Central (Hajipur)
  8. East Coast (Bhubaneswar)
  9. Southern (Chennai)
  10. South Central (Secunderabad)
  11. South Western (Hubballi)
  12. South Eastern (Kolkata)
  13. South East Central (Bilaspur)
  14. South Coast (Visakhapatnam) — newest, formed 2019
  15. Central (Mumbai CST)
  16. West Central (Jabalpur)
  17. Western (Mumbai Churchgate)
  18. Metro Railway (Kolkata)

Gauges

  • Broad gauge — 1,676 mm. Mission "Project Unigauge" has converted almost all of the network.
  • Metre gauge — 1,000 mm. Now very limited.
  • Narrow gauge — 762 mm or 610 mm. Mostly heritage lines.

Key trains & firsts

  • First train (1853) — 16 April 1853, Mumbai (Bori Bunder) to Thane. ~ 34 km.
  • Vande Bharat Express — semi-high-speed train. First launched 2019 (Delhi-Varanasi).
  • Rajdhani, Shatabdi, Duronto, Tejas, Gatimaan — premium trains.
  • Konkan Railway — Mumbai-Mangalore (~ 760 km), completed 1998. Major engineering feat through Western Ghats.
  • Mumbai-Ahmedabad bullet train — Japanese-tech, under construction; first stretch (Surat-Bilimora) targeted for 2026.

Heritage railways (UNESCO)

  • Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (1881)
  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway (1908) — Mettupalayam to Ooty, Tamil Nadu. Rack & pinion system.
  • Kalka-Shimla Railway (1903)
  • Matheran Hill Railway (1907)
  • Kangra Valley Railway

Inland Waterways.

India has ~ 14,500 km of navigable waterways. Maintained by the Inland Waterways Authority of India (IWAI). National Waterways (NWs) — 111 declared (NWAct 2016), 13 operational.

Ports — 12 major ports.

India has 12 major ports (managed by Central Govt) and over 200 minor ports (state). Major ports handle ~ 60% of total cargo. India's ports handled ~ 1,400 million tonnes of cargo in 2023-24.

13th major port — approved 2024, to be built at Dahanu near Mumbai. Will be one of the largest deep-water ports in India.

Civil aviation.

India is the 3rd largest aviation market in the world. About 153 operational airports including 34 international ones. Regulator: Directorate General of Civil Aviation (DGCA); airports are managed mostly by Airports Authority of India (AAI), with some by private operators (e.g. GMR Delhi/Hyderabad, GVK Mumbai).

Busiest airports (2024)

  1. Delhi (IGI) — busiest in India
  2. Mumbai (CSMI)
  3. Bengaluru (Kempegowda)
  4. Hyderabad (Rajiv Gandhi)
  5. Chennai
  6. Kolkata (NSCB)

Tamil Nadu's airports: Chennai (international, 2nd oldest in India), Coimbatore (international), Madurai (international), Tiruchirappalli (international), Tuticorin, Salem.

UDAN scheme (Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik) — launched 2017 to connect Tier 2/3 cities with cheap fares.

Pipelines.

Major oil and gas pipelines:

  • Naharkatiya–Noonmati–Barauni — oldest crude oil pipeline (1962); Assam to Bihar.
  • Salaya–Mathura — Gujarat to UP.
  • Mumbai High–Mumbai–Ankleshwar — offshore to mainland.
  • Hazira–Vijaipur–Jagdishpur (HVJ) — major gas pipeline; Gujarat to UP.
  • Dahej–Vijaipur, Vijaipur–Kota, Dabhol–Bengaluru.

Communication infrastructure.

  • Telephone subscribers (TRAI 2024): ~ 1.2 billion mobile subscribers — among the largest in the world. Reliance Jio, Bharti Airtel, Vodafone Idea, BSNL.
  • Internet subscribers: ~ 950 million.
  • 5G: Rolled out from October 2022. Over 200,000+ 5G BTS by 2024.
  • BharatNet — fibre to all 2.5 lakh gram panchayats; one of the world's largest rural broadband projects.
  • Satellites for communication: GSAT series; INSAT series.

Practice MCQs

CH 15 · 10 QUESTIONS
01The longest National Highway in India is —
  1. NH-1
  2. NH-44
  3. NH-7
  4. NH-15
Reveal answer

B — NH-44 (Srinagar → Kanyakumari, ~ 3,745 km).

02NW-1 connects —
  1. Sadiya & Dhubri
  2. Allahabad & Haldia
  3. Kollam & Kottapuram
  4. Krishna & Godavari
Reveal answer

B — Allahabad & Haldia (Ganga-Bhagirathi-Hugli system, ~ 1,620 km).

03India's first passenger train ran (16 April 1853) between —
  1. Howrah & Hooghly
  2. Madras & Arcot
  3. Mumbai & Thane
  4. Karachi & Kotri
Reveal answer

C — Mumbai (Bori Bunder) & Thane.

04The largest container port of India is —
  1. Mumbai
  2. Chennai
  3. JNPT
  4. Kolkata
Reveal answer

C — JNPT (Nhava Sheva, Maharashtra) — ~ 55% of India's container traffic.

05India's only riverine major port is —
  1. Mumbai
  2. Cochin
  3. Kolkata
  4. Paradip
Reveal answer

C — Kolkata (Syama Prasad Mookerjee Port, on the Hugli).

06Nilgiri Mountain Railway — a UNESCO World Heritage site — runs from —
  1. Coimbatore to Ooty
  2. Mettupalayam to Ooty
  3. Coonoor to Ooty
  4. Madurai to Kodaikanal
Reveal answer

B — Mettupalayam to Ooty, rack & pinion system, opened 1908.

07The first corporatised port in India is —
  1. Tuticorin
  2. Visakhapatnam
  3. Ennore
  4. JNPT
Reveal answer

C — Ennore (now Kamarajar Port, 1999).

08India's first oil pipeline (1962) ran from —
  1. Mumbai to Mathura
  2. Naharkatiya to Barauni
  3. Hazira to Vijaipur
  4. Salaya to Mathura
Reveal answer

B — Naharkatiya (Assam) to Barauni (Bihar).

09How many railway zones does Indian Railways have at present?
  1. 14
  2. 16
  3. 17
  4. 18
Reveal answer

D — 18. South Coast (Visakhapatnam) added in 2019.

10"UDAN" scheme stands for —
  1. United Domestic Aviation Network
  2. Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik
  3. Unified Departures and Network
  4. None of the above
Reveal answer

B — Ude Desh ka Aam Nagrik. Launched 2017 for regional connectivity.
