CHAPTER 06 / PHYSIOGRAPHY · COASTS · ISLANDS · DESERTS

Coasts, Islands & Deserts.

Two long coasts of different character, two island groups of different origin, and one great desert — the outer edges of India.

OBJECTIVES OF THIS CHAPTER

  • Distinguish the Western and Eastern coastal plains in width, slope, deltas, lagoons, and ports.
  • Name the regional sections of each coast and locate them.
  • Differentiate Lakshadweep (coralline) from Andaman & Nicobar (volcanic / tectonic).
  • Understand the formation, climate, and human geography of the Thar Desert.
  • Recall key facts for MCQs: India's only active volcano, only floating national park, India's longest lagoon, etc.

The Peninsular Plateau ends in two long coastal plains, the Western and the Eastern, which meet at Kanyakumari. They are different in almost every meaningful way — width, character of the rivers, the kind of ports they host, even the deltas they form. India also has two island groups: the Lakshadweep in the Arabian Sea, made entirely of coral; and the Andaman & Nicobar in the Bay of Bengal, of volcanic and tectonic origin. And in the country's northwest stretches the Thar Desert — the most densely populated desert in the world. This chapter rounds off our tour of Indian physiography.

The Western Coastal Plain.

Runs along the Arabian Sea coast from the Gulf of Khambhat in Gujarat to Kanyakumari in Tamil Nadu — about 1,500 km long. It is narrow (50–100 km), bounded sharply by the Western Ghats which rise abruptly inland. Because rivers descending the Ghats are short and swift, they do not form deltas — instead they make estuaries. The coast has many natural harbours (because of submergence in geological time), and almost all of India's largest natural ports are here.

KEY FACT — VEMBANAD LAKE

Vembanad Lake in Kerala is the longest lake in India (~ 96 km long, ~ 2,033 km² area at peak). It is part of the Malabar backwaters and is a Ramsar wetland site. The Kerala houseboats (kettuvallam) ply here. Other notable lagoons on the Malabar: Ashtamudi, Sasthamcotta (freshwater).

The Eastern Coastal Plain.

Runs along the Bay of Bengal from West Bengal in the north to Kanyakumari in the south — about 1,900 km long. It is much wider (100–130 km) than the western coast and slopes gently from the Eastern Ghats to the sea. The major rivers — Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna, Cauvery — all form large deltas, making this coast the granary of the south.

TAMIL NADU CONNECTION

The Coromandel coast is essentially Tamil Nadu's coast (Andhra Pradesh holds a small part in the north). Key points:
• Pulicat Lake — India's second-largest brackish-water lagoon (after Chilika); straddles TN–AP border; Sriharikota is here.
• Cauvery delta — Tamil Nadu's rice bowl; Thanjavur–Tiruvarur–Nagapattinam districts.
• Adam's Bridge (Ram Setu) — Dhanushkodi (Rameswaram) to Mannar (Sri Lanka).
• Marina Beach — Chennai; one of the longest urban beaches in the world (~ 13 km).

Lakshadweep Islands.

An archipelago of 36 islands (of which 10 are inhabited) in the Arabian Sea, about 200–440 km off the Kerala coast. The name Lakshadweep means "a hundred thousand islands" in Sanskrit. Total area: only 32 km², but the territorial waters extend over 4,200 km². Capital: Kavaratti. The islands are coral atolls and reefs, all rising from the Chagos-Laccadive submarine ridge — none are volcanic.

Three island groups (north to south)

  1. Amindivi Islands — northernmost (Amini, Kadmat, Kiltan, Chetlat, Bitra)
  2. Laccadive Islands — central (Andrott, Kalpeni, Kavaratti, Agatti, Bangaram)
  3. Minicoy — southernmost, separated from the others by the Nine Degree Channel, and separated from the Maldives by the Eight Degree Channel
KEY FACT — LARGEST LAKSHADWEEP ISLAND

Andrott is the largest island of Lakshadweep by area (~ 4.8 km²) and the only one oriented east-west. Minicoy is the southernmost. Bitra is the smallest inhabited island.

Andaman & Nicobar Islands.

An archipelago of 572 islands (around 38 inhabited) in the Bay of Bengal, about 1,200 km from mainland India, stretching ~ 800 km from north to south. Total area: 8,249 km². Capital: Port Blair (on South Andaman). The islands are tectonic and volcanic in origin — extensions of the Arakan Yoma–Sumatra island arc. They are tectonically very active.

Two groups, separated by the Ten Degree Channel

  • Andaman Islands — northern. Four main islands (North, Middle, South, Little Andaman). South Andaman has Port Blair. The original inhabitants include the Sentinelese, Jarawa, Onge, and Great Andamanese tribes.
  • Nicobar Islands — southern. Largest island is Great Nicobar; Indira Point (6° 45′ N) on Great Nicobar is the southernmost point of India.

India's only active volcano

Barren Island in the Andaman group is the only active volcano in South Asia. It erupted again in 2017 after lying dormant for over a century. The dormant volcano Narcondam Island is also in this region.

India's highest peak in the islands

Saddle Peak (732 m) on North Andaman is the highest point of the islands.

TNPSC TIP — LAKSHADWEEP vs ANDAMAN

Confusion-trap: Lakshadweep is coral, Andaman is volcanic/tectonic. Lakshadweep is in the Arabian Sea (off Kerala); Andaman is in the Bay of Bengal (off the eastern coast). The Ten Degree Channel separates Andaman from Nicobar; the Six Degree Channel separates Nicobar from Indonesia; the Eight Degree Channel separates Minicoy from Maldives.

The Thar Desert.

India's only major desert, lying in north-western India and extending into Pakistan. Within India, it covers about 200,000 km² across Rajasthan (largest share), Gujarat, Punjab, and Haryana. Locally known as the Great Indian Desert or Marusthali ("land of the dead" in Sanskrit). It is the most densely populated desert in the world — about 83 people/km² compared with around 7/km² for the Sahara.

Formation

The Thar is a hot subtropical desert. Its origin is unique. The monsoon winds reaching this region from the south-west blow parallel to the Aravallis, not perpendicular to them — so the Aravallis cannot force the winds to rise and release rain. As a result, less than 150 mm/year of rainfall falls over most of the desert. Sand dunes called barchans shift with the strong winds.

Topography

  • Sandy plain — covers most of the desert; dunes up to 150 m high.
  • Rocky outcrops — bhakar, magra (Aravalli outliers).
  • Playas / saline lakes — Sambhar (India's largest inland salt lake), Didwana, Kuchaman, Pachpadra. Source of common salt.
  • Rann of Kachchh — saline marsh straddling the Gujarat–Pakistan border. Splits into the Great Rann (north) and Little Rann (east). Famous for wild ass (Indian onager) sanctuary; world-famous Rann Utsav held annually.

The Luni and ephemeral rivers

The Luni is the only major river of the Thar; it originates near Ajmer and flows southwest for about 495 km before disappearing into the Rann of Kachchh — it never reaches the sea. The Luni's water becomes saline downstream. Other small streams (Ghaggar) are seasonal.

People & economy

Despite its harshness, the Thar is densely settled. Camel-based herding, millets (bajra), pulses, oilseeds. Wind and solar energy potential is huge — the Bhadla Solar Park in Rajasthan is one of the largest solar installations in the world. The Indira Gandhi Canal, drawing water from Punjab, has transformed parts of western Rajasthan into wheat country.

MNEMONIC — TWO RANNS

Great Rann = north of the highway; Little Rann = east of it. Wild ass sanctuary = Little Rann. Rann Utsav = held at Dhordo, near the Great Rann.

QUICK RECAP

What this chapter said.

  • Western Coast — narrow, submerged, estuaries, many natural ports. Sections: Kachchh, Kathiawar, Konkan, Kanara, Malabar.
  • Eastern Coast — wider, emerged, deltas, fewer natural ports. Sections: Utkal, Northern Circars, Coromandel.
  • Lakshadweep — 36 coral atolls in the Arabian Sea; capital Kavaratti; Minicoy southernmost.
  • Andaman & Nicobar — 572 volcanic/tectonic islands; capital Port Blair; Indira Point southernmost; Saddle Peak highest; Barren Island is India's only active volcano.
  • Ten Degree Channel — Andaman/Nicobar; Eight Degree — Lakshadweep/Maldives; Six Degree — Great Nicobar/Sumatra.
  • Thar Desert — ~ 200,000 km² in India, most densely populated desert; Luni only major river; Sambhar largest inland salt lake.
  • Rann of Kachchh — saline marsh; Great Rann + Little Rann; wild ass sanctuary in Little Rann.

Practice MCQs

CH 06 · 10 QUESTIONS
01India's only active volcano is located on —
  1. Narcondam Island
  2. Barren Island
  3. Great Nicobar
  4. Saddle Peak
Reveal answer

B — Barren Island in the Andaman group. Last major eruption phase: 2017.

02The Coromandel coast lies along which body of water?
  1. Arabian Sea
  2. Bay of Bengal
  3. Indian Ocean only
  4. Gulf of Mannar only
Reveal answer

B — Bay of Bengal. The Coromandel runs along southern AP and Tamil Nadu.

03Vembanad Lake — the longest lake in India — is part of the —
  1. Konkan coast
  2. Kanara coast
  3. Malabar coast
  4. Coromandel coast
Reveal answer

C — Malabar coast, Kerala. ~ 96 km long; Ramsar wetland.

04Lakshadweep islands are made entirely of —
  1. Basalt
  2. Granite
  3. Coral
  4. Sandstone
Reveal answer

C — Coral. They rise from the Chagos-Laccadive submarine ridge.

05The Ten Degree Channel separates —
  1. Andaman from Nicobar
  2. Lakshadweep from Maldives
  3. India from Sri Lanka
  4. Minicoy from the rest of Lakshadweep
Reveal answer

A — Andaman from Nicobar.

06The largest inland salt lake of India is —
  1. Sambhar
  2. Chilika
  3. Pulicat
  4. Loktak
Reveal answer

A — Sambhar (Rajasthan). Chilika and Pulicat are brackish coastal lagoons; Loktak is a freshwater lake.

07The only major river of the Thar, which never reaches the sea, is —
  1. Mahi
  2. Sabarmati
  3. Luni
  4. Banas
Reveal answer

C — Luni. It rises in the Aravalli and dies in the Rann of Kachchh.

08The Western Coastal Plain forms primarily — at its river mouths —
  1. Deltas
  2. Estuaries
  3. Lagoons
  4. Barrier islands
Reveal answer

B — Estuaries. Short, swift, sediment-poor rivers in a submerged coast produce estuaries, not deltas.

09India's southernmost point — Indira Point — is on —
  1. South Andaman
  2. Great Nicobar
  3. Minicoy
  4. Saddle Peak
Reveal answer

B — Great Nicobar, at 6° 45′ N. Earlier called Pygmalion Point.

10Which of the following is in the Eastern Coastal Plain?
  1. Konkan
  2. Malabar
  3. Northern Circars
  4. Kanara
Reveal answer

C — Northern Circars (Andhra Pradesh). The others belong to the Western Coast.
