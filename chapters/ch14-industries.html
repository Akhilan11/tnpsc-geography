CHAPTER 14 / INDUSTRIES

Industries.

From the steel mills of Jamshedpur to the IT corridors of Bengaluru and Chennai — how India makes things.

OBJECTIVES

  • Identify factors influencing industrial location.
  • Map the major industries — iron-steel, textiles, sugar, cement, chemicals, automobile, IT.
  • Memorise plant locations and leading-producer states.
  • Locate India's six major industrial regions.

Factors of industrial location.

  • Raw materials — heavy industries near sources.
  • Power — coal, electricity, gas.
  • Labour — skilled (IT, electronics) vs unskilled.
  • Transport — rail, road, port, pipeline.
  • Markets — perishable industries (food) near consumers.
  • Capital & finance — Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Bengaluru.
  • Government policy — SEZs, incentives, freight equalisation (historical).
  • Water — heavy water user industries (textile, chemicals, paper).

Iron and steel industry.

The most basic of heavy industries. India is the world's 2nd largest crude steel producer (after China). National capacity: ~ 161 MT (2024). Major integrated steel plants:

Cotton textile industry.

The first modern industry in India (Bombay 1854 — Cowasjee Davar). India is the world's 2nd largest producer of cotton textiles. Major centres: Maharashtra (Mumbai, Pune, Sholapur, Nagpur), Gujarat (Ahmedabad — "Manchester of India", Surat, Vadodara), Tamil Nadu (Coimbatore — "Manchester of South India", Tiruppur — knitwear capital, Madurai, Salem, Karur), West Bengal (Kolkata, Howrah), Punjab (Ludhiana — hosiery).

Jute industry.

Concentrated almost entirely in the Hugli basin: West Bengal (~ 90% of mills). The industry was historically dominated by Scottish capital — now mostly state-owned (Jute Corporation of India). Bangladesh's partition in 1947 hurt the industry: jute fields went to East Pakistan but mills stayed in India.

Sugar industry.

India is the largest sugar producer in the world (some years; alternates with Brazil). Industry shifting from north (UP, Bihar) to south & west (Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu) because of higher sucrose content and longer crushing season. Top states: UP, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu.

Cement industry.

India is the 2nd largest cement producer in the world (~ 8% of global production). Need limestone + coal/power. Major plants:

  • Rajasthan — largest producer; many plants in Chittaurgarh, Sirohi.
  • Andhra Pradesh & Telangana — Vijaywada region.
  • Tamil Nadu — Ariyalur, Dalmiapuram, Sankaridurg.
  • Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka, Gujarat, Chhattisgarh — major producers.

First cement plant in India: Porbandar, Gujarat, 1904.

Chemicals, fertilizers, petrochemicals.

Fertilizers

India is the 3rd largest fertilizer producer in the world. Key plants: Sindri (Jharkhand, India's first 1951), Trombay, Namrup (Assam — natural gas), Vadodara, Kanpur, Nangal, Coromandel (Visakhapatnam), Mangalore.

Petrochemicals

Concentrated at: Reliance Jamnagar; IOC Panipat; Mangalore Chemicals & Fertilizers; Vadodara IPCL; Manali (Chennai).

Chemicals

Vadodara, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, Vapi (Gujarat), Cuddalore, Manali (TN), Hyderabad (Pharma — bulk drugs).

Automobile industry.

India is the 3rd largest automobile market in the world (passenger + commercial). Hubs:

  • Chennai — "Detroit of India" — Hyundai, Ford (former), Renault-Nissan, BMW, Daimler, Royal Enfield (Tiruvottiyur), Ashok Leyland, TVS, Hindustan Motors. Largest auto exporter in India.
  • Delhi NCR (Gurugram, Manesar, Noida) — Maruti Suzuki, Honda, Hero MotoCorp, Yamaha.
  • Pune-Aurangabad-Nashik (Maharashtra) — Tata Motors, Mahindra, Bajaj, Fiat, Mercedes-Benz, Volkswagen.
  • Hosur (TN) & Bengaluru (Karnataka) — Toyota Kirloskar, TVS, Ola Electric.
  • Sanand (Gujarat) — Tata Nano (former), Ford, Maruti.
  • Jamshedpur — Tata Motors trucks.
TAMIL NADU CONNECTION — DETROIT OF INDIA

Tamil Nadu has the largest concentration of auto and auto-component industries in India. Chennai's Ennore-Tiruvallur-Sriperumbudur belt, combined with Hosur, produces nearly 30% of India's cars and 35% of its commercial vehicles. Major manufacturers: Hyundai (largest), Renault-Nissan, BMW, Daimler/Bharat Benz, Ashok Leyland, TVS, Royal Enfield, Ford (factory now Tata's).

IT & electronics industry.

India is the world's largest IT-BPM exporter. Total IT sector revenue (2024): ~ $250 billion, of which ~ $200 billion is exports. The biggest hubs:

  • Bengaluru — "Silicon Valley of India" — Karnataka.
  • Hyderabad — "Cyberabad" — Telangana.
  • Chennai & Tamil Nadu (Coimbatore, Tiruchi) — major IT corridor (OMR/Old Mahabalipuram Road).
  • Gurugram, Noida, Delhi NCR.
  • Pune (Maharashtra).
  • Kolkata, Bhubaneswar, Kochi/Thiruvananthapuram, Mumbai.

Major Indian IT companies: TCS (Tata, headquartered Mumbai), Infosys (Bengaluru), Wipro (Bengaluru), HCL (Noida), Tech Mahindra (Pune).

Electronics manufacturing

India is the world's 2nd largest mobile phone manufacturer. Major hubs: Sriperumbudur (TN — Foxconn for Apple/iPhone), Noida (Samsung — largest factory in world), Bengaluru, Hyderabad.

Six major industrial regions of India.

  1. Mumbai-Pune region — Maharashtra. Cotton textiles, chemicals, petrochem, autos, films.
  2. Hugli region — West Bengal. Jute, paper, engineering, chemicals.
  3. Bengaluru-Chennai region — Karnataka & Tamil Nadu. Textiles, autos, electronics, IT.
  4. Gujarat region — Ahmedabad-Vadodara-Surat-Jamnagar. Textiles, chemicals, petrochem, diamonds.
  5. Chotanagpur region — Jharkhand, WB, Odisha, Chhattisgarh. Iron & steel, heavy engineering.
  6. Delhi NCR region — Delhi, Gurugram, Noida, Faridabad, Ghaziabad. Autos, electronics, ready-made garments.

Industrial corridors (government initiative)

  • Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor (DMIC)
  • Chennai-Bengaluru Industrial Corridor (CBIC)
  • Amritsar-Kolkata Industrial Corridor (AKIC)
  • Vizag-Chennai Industrial Corridor (VCIC)
  • Bengaluru-Mumbai Economic Corridor (BMEC)
  • East Coast Economic Corridor (ECEC)

01India's first integrated iron and steel plant was set up in —
  1. Burnpur
  2. Bhadravati
  3. Jamshedpur
  4. Durgapur

C — Jamshedpur (TISCO, 1907).

02"Manchester of South India" is —
  1. Madurai
  2. Coimbatore
  3. Chennai
  4. Salem
Reveal answer

B — Coimbatore. Ahmedabad is "Manchester of India".

03The "Detroit of India" is —
  1. Pune
  2. Delhi
  3. Chennai
  4. Jamshedpur
Reveal answer

C — Chennai.

04Bhilai Steel Plant was set up with the collaboration of —
  1. Germany
  2. UK
  3. USSR
  4. Japan
Reveal answer

C — USSR (Soviet collaboration, 1955). Rourkela was German; Durgapur was British; Bokaro was Soviet.

05India's first cement plant (1904) was at —
  1. Surat
  2. Porbandar
  3. Mumbai
  4. Karachi
Reveal answer

B — Porbandar (Gujarat).

06The "Silicon Valley of India" is —
  1. Hyderabad
  2. Pune
  3. Bengaluru
  4. Chennai
Reveal answer

C — Bengaluru. Hyderabad is "Cyberabad".

07India's first cotton textile mill (1854) was at —
  1. Surat
  2. Ahmedabad
  3. Mumbai
  4. Madras
Reveal answer

C — Mumbai, by Cowasjee Nanabhoy Davar.

08The major jute industry of India is concentrated in —
  1. Brahmaputra valley
  2. Hugli basin
  3. Krishna delta
  4. Cauvery delta
Reveal answer

B — Hugli basin (West Bengal).

09Salem Steel Plant specialises in —
  1. Pig iron
  2. Mild steel
  3. Stainless steel
  4. Rails
Reveal answer

C — Stainless steel.

10"Tiruppur" in Tamil Nadu is famous as the —
  1. Cement capital
  2. Knitwear / hosiery capital
  3. Leather capital
  4. Granite capital
Reveal answer

B — Knitwear capital of India. Largest source of cotton knitwear exports.
