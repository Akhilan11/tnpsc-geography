CHAPTER 27 / ENVIRONMENT

Environmental Issues.

The defining challenges of the 21st century — and where India stands in the global response.

OBJECTIVES

  • Understand global warming, climate change, and the greenhouse effect.
  • Distinguish ozone depletion from global warming.
  • Recall key environmental agreements: Stockholm, Rio, Kyoto, Paris.
  • Know major Indian environmental laws and the bodies (MoEFCC, CPCB, NGT).

Global warming & climate change.

Greenhouse effect — natural process by which atmospheric gases trap heat. Without it, Earth would be ~ 33°C cooler. But human emissions have intensified the effect.

Key greenhouse gases

  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂) — primary; from fossil fuels, deforestation.
  • Methane (CH₄) — 25x more potent than CO₂; from livestock, paddy, landfills.
  • Nitrous oxide (N₂O) — from fertilizers, industry.
  • Fluorinated gases (HFCs, CFCs, PFCs, SF₆) — synthetic; very high warming potential.
  • Water vapour — the most abundant; amplifies other warming.

Observed impacts

  • Global mean temperature has risen by ~ 1.2°C above pre-industrial (IPCC AR6, 2023).
  • Sea level has risen ~ 21-24 cm since 1880; accelerating.
  • Arctic sea ice declining ~ 13% per decade.
  • Glacier retreat: Gangotri retreating ~ 22 m/year recently.
  • More intense heatwaves, droughts, floods, cyclones.
  • Ocean acidification (pH dropped ~ 0.1 since pre-industrial).

Ozone layer depletion.

Different from global warming. The ozone layer (15-30 km altitude) absorbs UV-B and UV-C radiation. Depletion is caused by chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), halons, methyl bromide. A seasonal "Antarctic ozone hole" forms each spring. The Montreal Protocol (1987) phased out ozone-depleting substances; the ozone layer is now slowly healing — projected full recovery by ~ 2066.

Pollution.

Air pollution

  • Indicators: PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, O₃, CO.
  • India has 14 of the 20 most polluted cities in the world (IQAir 2023).
  • National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) — launched 2019; target 40% reduction in PM2.5/PM10 by 2026 (revised).
  • Major sources: vehicle emissions, industrial, construction dust, agricultural burning, household biomass burning, brick kilns, thermal plants.

Water pollution

  • Pathogens, sewage, industrial effluents, agricultural runoff.
  • Indicator: BOD (biological oxygen demand), DO (dissolved oxygen), heavy metals.
  • India's Ganga is among the world's most polluted; Namami Gange Mission (2014) for cleaning.
  • Eutrophication — nutrient overload causing algal blooms; e.g. Hussain Sagar.

Soil pollution

Heavy metals (Pb, Cd, Hg, As), pesticides, plastics, e-waste.

Noise pollution

Threshold: 65 dB (day), 55 dB (night) in residential zones (CPCB).

Light pollution

Recent recognition; affects ecology and astronomy.

Deforestation.

  • World loses ~ 10 million ha of forest per year (FAO).
  • Major drivers: agriculture expansion, mining, urbanisation, fires.
  • Amazon and Congo forests are the most-watched biomes.
  • India's net forest cover: 21.76% (ISFR 2023). Slight gains over the last decade (some debate over the inclusion of plantations).

Biodiversity loss.

  • ~ 1 million species at risk of extinction (IPBES 2019).
  • "Sixth mass extinction" anthropogenic.
  • Habitat loss, fragmentation, invasive species, pollution, climate change, overhunting.
  • India is among 17 megadiverse countries; 4 of 36 biodiversity hotspots intersect India.

Solid waste & plastics.

  • India generates ~ 1.5 lakh tonnes municipal solid waste per day. About 30% remains uncollected.
  • Plastic waste: ~ 3.4 million tonnes/year. Single-use plastic ban came into effect July 1, 2022.
  • E-waste: India is among top 5 global generators.
  • Hazardous waste — biomedical, radioactive — regulated separately.

India's environmental laws.

  • Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 — Schedule I species (max protection), national parks, sanctuaries.
  • Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 — CPCB/SPCBs.
  • Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 — restricts diversion of forest land.
  • Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981.
  • Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 — passed after Bhopal disaster (1984); umbrella law; allows EIA notifications.
  • Public Liability Insurance Act, 1991.
  • National Environment Tribunal Act, 1995 — replaced by NGT Act.
  • Biological Diversity Act, 2002 — National Biodiversity Authority (NBA), Chennai.
  • Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 — "Forest Rights Act".
  • National Green Tribunal Act, 2010 — NGT (HQ Delhi, 4 zonal benches: Bhopal, Chennai, Pune, Kolkata).
  • Energy Conservation Act, 2001 & amendments.
  • Mission LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment) — India's flagship behavioural campaign launched at COP27 (2022).
KEY FACT — INDIA'S CLIMATE COMMITMENTS

India's "Panchamrit" (5 commitments at COP26 Glasgow 2021):
1. 500 GW of non-fossil energy capacity by 2030.
2. 50% of energy from renewables by 2030.
3. Reduce projected emissions by 1 billion tonnes by 2030.
4. Reduce carbon intensity of GDP by 45% from 2005 levels by 2030.
5. Achieve net-zero by 2070.

01The Montreal Protocol (1987) deals with —
  1. Climate change
  2. Ozone depletion
  3. Biodiversity
  4. Hazardous waste
Reveal answer

B — Ozone depletion. Phases out CFCs and other ODS.

02India's net-zero target year is —
  1. 2050
  2. 2060
  3. 2070
  4. 2080
Reveal answer

C — 2070 (announced by PM Modi at COP26 Glasgow).

03The National Green Tribunal (NGT) was established in —
  1. 2002
  2. 2006
  3. 2010
  4. 2014
Reveal answer

C — 2010. NGT Act 2010; headquartered Delhi.

04UNEP's headquarters is in —
  1. New York
  2. Geneva
  3. Nairobi
  4. Vienna
Reveal answer

C — Nairobi, Kenya.

05India's Environment Protection Act was passed in —
  1. 1972
  2. 1980
  3. 1986
  4. 1992
Reveal answer

C — 1986 — after the Bhopal Gas Tragedy of 1984.

06The Paris Agreement was adopted in —
  1. 2009
  2. 2012
  3. 2015
  4. 2018
Reveal answer

C — 2015 (COP21).

07The greenhouse gas with highest global warming potential per molecule among the common GHGs is —
  1. CO₂
  2. CH₄
  3. N₂O
  4. SF₆
Reveal answer

D — SF₆ (sulphur hexafluoride) — ~ 23,500x CO₂ over 100 years. Among common gases CO₂ < CH₄ < N₂O < SF₆.

08India's National Biodiversity Authority (NBA) is headquartered in —
  1. Delhi

  3. Chennai
  4. Hyderabad

C — Chennai.

09India's renewable energy target for 2030 is —
  1. 300 GW
  2. 400 GW
  3. 500 GW (non-fossil)
  4. 750 GW
Reveal answer

C — 500 GW non-fossil capacity by 2030 (Panchamrit).

10India's ban on single-use plastic items became effective from —
  1. January 1, 2020
  2. July 1, 2022
  3. January 1, 2023
